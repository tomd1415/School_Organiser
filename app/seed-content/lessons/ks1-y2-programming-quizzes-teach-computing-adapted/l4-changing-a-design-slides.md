# Changing a design

## Today we are learning
- choose backgrounds for the design
- choose characters for the design
- create a program based on the new design

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: sprite · project · blocks · design · sequence · change. Same routine as always.

## Last lesson
- a design tells you what to build
- the Start on tap block runs when you tap the sprite

> 🧑‍🏫 Quick recap. Ask one pupil what a design shows.

## A quiz question
![The "Who lives here?" quiz]({{res:l4-the-who-lives-here-quiz-screen-teach-computing.png}})

- the question is "**Who lives here?**"
- the background is the **sea**
- you tap the right answer — the **fish**

> 🧑‍🏫 The starter labels the cat, the fish and the program. Ask which animal lives in the sea.

## Right answer
![The right-answer code]({{res:l4-the-right-answer-code-says-yes-teach-computing.png}})

- tap the **right** answer
- it says "**Yes**"
- then it changes to the next question

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. Walk through the blocks slowly, left to right.

## Wrong answer
![The wrong-answer code]({{res:l4-the-wrong-answer-code-says-no-teach-computing.png}})

- tap the **wrong** answer
- it says "**No**"
- then the program **stops**

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Compare the two codes. Likely error: thinking the wrong answer also changes the background → fix-words "the wrong answer just says No and stops."

## Choose your own
- pick a **background** (somewhere real)
- pick **two sprites** that live there
- one is the right answer, one is the wrong answer

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Pupils record choices on the activity sheet, then build the question and screenshot it. Support: reuse the sea. Challenge: a harder background.
