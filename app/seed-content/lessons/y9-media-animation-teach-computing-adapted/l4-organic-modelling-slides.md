# Organic modelling

## Today we are learning
- use proportional editing
- use the knife tool
- use subdivision

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Everyone has Blender open.

## Starter — name the parts
A model is made of **vertices**, **edges**, and **faces**. Label them on the starter worksheet.

> 🧑‍🏫 Take answers, then point each part out on a live model.

## Real things are not perfect
![A symmetrical face and a natural, asymmetrical face]({{res:l4-symmetry-faces.png}})

Natural things are usually a little bit **asymmetrical**. Perfectly even models look fake.

> 🧑‍🏫 Discuss how the right-hand face is uneven: parting off-centre, eyes at different heights.

## Proportional editing  (I do)
Turn it on. Move one point and the points **nearby** move too — like bending clay.

> 🧑‍🏫 Demo on a cylinder to bend a tree trunk. Likely error: nothing bends — fix-words "check the strength is turned up."

## The knife tool  (we do → you do)
Cut a shape into a flat surface, press **Enter** to fix the cut, delete the waste.

> 🧑‍🏫 Make a palm leaf. Support: steps in order on the worksheet. Challenge: add coconuts. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Subdivision
![A finished palm tree]({{res:l4-palm-tree.png}})

Subdivision splits one face into many smaller ones, so you can shape it more smoothly.

> 🧑‍🏫 Make a desert island with subdivision + proportional editing. Hide the tree in the Outliner first.
