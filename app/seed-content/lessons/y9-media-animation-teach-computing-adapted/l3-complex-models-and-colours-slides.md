# Complex models and colours

## Today we are learning
- use edit mode and extrude
- use loop cut and face editing
- put different colours on one model

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Everyone has Blender open.

## Starter — real or computer made?
3D models are built from **vertices** (corners), **edges** (lines), and **faces** (flat surfaces).

> 🧑‍🏫 Discuss: a 3D model can look real. Match the three building blocks on the starter worksheet.

## Edit mode  (I do)
Press **Tab** to enter edit mode. Now you can move the corners, lines, and faces of a shape.

> 🧑‍🏫 Demo: add a cylinder, press Tab, show vertex/edge/face select. Likely error: tools missing — fix-words "you are not in edit mode, press Tab."

## Extrude — pull out a new part  (we do)
![A grey rocket made by extruding a cylinder]({{res:l3-grey-rocket-extrude.png}})

Select a face, press **E**, move the mouse. A new part grows out.

> 🧑‍🏫 Make a rocket: extrude the body up, extrude the nose, scale it in. Loop cut adds an edge ring to cut from.

## You can build anything  (you do)
![A chair model]({{res:l3-chair-model.png}})

Use extrude and loop cut to build your rocket — or a chair.

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: steps in order on the worksheet. Challenge (explorer): make a car. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Many colours on one model
![A rocket coloured with several materials]({{res:l3-rocket-with-several-materials.png}})

In edit mode, select some faces, pick a material, press **Assign**.

> 🧑‍🏫 Make three named materials (link to variables again). Select faces, Assign, repeat.

Tick your three "I can…". Show me your model.
