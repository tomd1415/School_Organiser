# Animation, names, parenting

## Today we are learning
- add, move, and delete keyframes
- play and move through the timeline
- give objects useful names
- join objects with parenting

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Open last lesson's snow scene, or the template snow scene.

## Starter — two kinds of animation
- **Stop motion:** move a real object, take a photo, repeat.
- **Keyframe:** set key positions; the computer fills the gaps.

> 🧑‍🏫 Teacher-played hook (optional): play ~10 seconds of an old stop-motion clip, then ask "why is keyframing faster on a computer?". Keep it short and calm. Sort the cards on the starter worksheet.

## Keyframes and tweening
![Keyframes (set positions) and the tweened frames between them]({{res:l2-keyframe-tween-diagram.png}})

The red ones are **keyframes** you set. The grey ones are **tweened** — the computer makes them.

> 🧑‍🏫 Link to computing: keyframing automates the in-between work, like an algorithm of steps.

## The timeline  (I do)
![The Blender timeline]({{res:l2-blender-timeline.png}})

Select an object, press **I** to add a keyframe, move the timeline, move the object, press **I** again.

> 🧑‍🏫 Common error: not selecting the object first, or no keyframe at the START. Fix-words "select it, then set a keyframe at frame 1."

## Your turn — animate one object  (you do)
Make the snowball roll into the snowman. Two keyframes, then play.

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: the steps are in order on the worksheet. Challenge: make the hat fall off, or bounce. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Names and parenting
![The Outliner with the Head parented to the Body]({{res:l2-parenting-outliner.png}})

Name your objects. **Parent** the head to the body so they move together.

> 🧑‍🏫 Drag Head onto Body in the Outliner while holding Shift. Move the body — the head follows.
