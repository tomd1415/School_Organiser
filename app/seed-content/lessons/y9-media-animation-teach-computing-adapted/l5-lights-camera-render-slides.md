## Lights  (I do)
- **Spot** = a cone of light (a lighthouse beam)
- **Sun** = lights everything evenly (daylight)
- **Point** = shines all around (a bulb)

![A night scene lit by a spotlight]({{res:l5-island-spotlight-night.png}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Add a spot to the lighthouse, turn up the power, animate it spinning. Likely error: light too weak — fix-words "turn up the power."

## The camera and the rule of thirds  (we do)
![A scene with a rule-of-thirds grid]({{res:l5-rule-of-thirds.png}})

Place your main object on a line, not in the dead centre.

> 🧑‍🏫 Press 0 to look through the camera. Use focus (f-stop) to blur the background.

## Your turn — light and frame your scene  (you do)
Add a light, set up the camera, choose a render mode.

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: steps in order on the worksheet. Challenge: animate the sun turning red as it sets. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Render modes
Fast preview mode vs **ray tracing** (Cycles) — realistic light and shadows, but slow.

> 🧑‍🏫 Ray tracing stresses the computer. Use the fast mode while building; render in Cycles for the final shot.
