## Keyframes and tweening
The **keyframes** are the positions you set. The frames the computer makes in between are **tweened** (the in-between frames). Drag the right label onto each spot.

```label
image: {{res:l2-keyframe-tween-diagram.png}}
first set position (5%, 84%): keyframe
an in-between frame (22%, 38%): tweened
another set position (42%, 9%): keyframe
another in-between frame (75%, 35%): tweened
```

## The timeline
The timeline at the bottom of Blender stores your keyframes. The yellow diamond is a keyframe.

## 🟡 Core
Put the steps to make a keyframe animation in the right order.

```order
Select the object you want to animate
Go to frame 1 and press I to add a keyframe
Move along the timeline to a later frame
Move the object to its new position
Press I to add another keyframe
Press play to watch the animation
```
