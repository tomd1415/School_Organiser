# Build it up

## Today we are learning
- build a prototype that follows our plan
- use a "wait for the button" pattern
- give and use peer feedback
- record a problem and a fix in our diary

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: prototype · refine · feedback · decomposition · debug. Pairs need their proposal, their code and their project diary open.

## Starter — wait for the button
A loop that **waits for one event and then stops** — handy for starting a game or a project.

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the starter Parsons. Point out alternative correct answers exist. Mention `running_time()` for measuring how long something took — useful in projects.

## The journey of a project
design → build → get feedback → refine → finish

> 🧑‍🏫 Show pupils where they are: today is BUILD and first FEEDBACK. They will have a little more time next lesson, so plan what to finish first.

## You do — build your prototype
Work from your proposal. Build one feature at a time and test it on the board.

> 🧑‍🏫 Circulate and observe — this is your main assessment window. Prompt, do not fix it for them. Common stall: trying everything at once → "get ONE feature working first".

## Pause — feedback and diary  (we do)
1. tell another pair about your prototype
2. write their feedback in your diary
3. write how you will use it

> 🧑‍🏫 Remind them what good feedback is: a specific strength and a specific improvement. Not all feedback is actionable — that is fine, they say why.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…" and rate your confidence on the worksheet.

> 🧑‍🏫 The confidence slider is just for them — no marks. Ask the room: "what has been hardest?" and share fixes.
