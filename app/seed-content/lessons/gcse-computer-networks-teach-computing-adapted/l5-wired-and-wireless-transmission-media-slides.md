## Transmission media  (I do)
**Transmission media** = how the data travels. Two kinds:
- **Wired** — a physical cable.
- **Wireless** — signals through the air.

![A wired network cable]({{res:l5-a-network-cable-with-an-rj45-connector.png}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Plain line: wired = cable, wireless = air. Then the wired/wireless card-sort. Stress: everything carries the same 0s and 1s, just different ways.

## The four media  (we do)
- **Copper** — wired, electrical signals, cheap, fine over short distances.
- **Fibre optic** — wired, light pulses, fast over long distances, costs more.
- **WiFi** — wireless radio, many devices, about 50 m.
- **Bluetooth** — wireless radio, short range about 10 m, low power.

> 🧑‍🏫 Class matches each media to its description (Support). Likely error: thinking fibre is wireless because it's "fast" — fix-words "fibre is a cable full of light."

## Choose the media  (you do)
▶ **Your turn — open your activity worksheet now.** Choose the best transmission media for each scenario.

- Big files, fast, stable → **fibre**.
- Moving around, sharing internet → **WiFi**.
- Tiny short-range gadgets (mouse, headset) → **Bluetooth**.
