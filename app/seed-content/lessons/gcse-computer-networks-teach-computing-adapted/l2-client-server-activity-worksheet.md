## What we are learning
Today I will describe the role of a client and a server, compare client–server with peer-to-peer, and describe a PAN, a LAN and a WAN.

![A server — a computer that answers requests from clients]({{res:l2-server.png}})

In the **client–server model** there are two roles. A **client** asks for things. A **server** answers the requests and sends them back.

## Client or server?
Drag each job into the right box.

```sort
The client (asks): your laptop or phone, sends a request, displays the file you asked for
The server (answers): stores the shared files, waits for requests, sends the file back to the client
```

## PAN, LAN or WAN?
Networks come in three sizes. Drag each example into the right box.

```sort
PAN — around one person: a smartwatch linked to a phone, a hands-free headset, a wireless mouse and laptop
LAN — one site or building: a home network, a single-site school network, an office full of connected PCs
WAN — a large area: the internet, a bank's offices in many cities, a national CCTV system
```
