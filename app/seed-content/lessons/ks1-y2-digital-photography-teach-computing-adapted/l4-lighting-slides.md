# Lighting

## Today we are learning
- see how light changes a photo
- find a place with good light
- use a different light source
- say why a photo is unclear

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: light source · flash · focus · background. Recap: positioning, framing, detail.

## Keep or delete?
![A bright, clear photo of a tree]({{res:l4-a-bright-clear-photo-of-a-tree-teach-computing.png}})
![A photo that is too dark]({{res:l4-a-photo-that-is-too-dark-teach-computing.jpg}})

- a clear photo → keep
- a dark or blurry photo → delete
- we do not keep every photo we take

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. On the worksheet pupils SORT photos into keep / delete. It is fine to have different opinions.

## Why is good light important?
![A clear, well-lit photo of food]({{res:l4-a-clear-well-lit-photo-of-food-teach-computing.png}})

- cameras need **enough light**
- our eyes see better than the camera
- too little light → a dark photo

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. Show the dark photo again. Explain: check there is enough light before you press.

## Find the best light
- try the windowsill, under a table, the hall, outside
- photograph the **same object** each time
- only the **light** changes

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Turn off auto-flash for this test. TA: help pupils keep the same object and angle each time.

## A different light source
- move to a brighter place, **or**
- turn on the camera **flash**, **or**
- shine a **torch** on the object

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. On the worksheet pupils ORDER the steps to fix a dark photo. Likely error: covering the flash with a finger → fix-words "keep your fingers away from the flash."
