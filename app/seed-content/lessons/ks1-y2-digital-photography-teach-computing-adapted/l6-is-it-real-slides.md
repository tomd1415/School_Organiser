# Is it real?

## Today we are learning
- use my photo skills to take a good photo
- see that photos can be changed
- spot clues that a photo has been changed
- decide if a photo is real or changed

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: format · framing · lighting · focus · filter. This is the last lesson — recap the whole unit.

## Use everything you have learned
- portrait or landscape?
- good framing?
- good lighting?
- clear and in focus?

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1. Pupils take their best photo of the classroom using every skill. Remind: ask before photographing a person; check the background.

## Photos can be changed
![A real photo of a dog and a baby giraffe]({{res:l6-real-dog-giraffe.png}})
![A changed photo with a whale added near a bridge]({{res:l6-changed-whale.png}})

- some photos are **real**
- some are **changed** on a computer
- last lesson you changed the colour of a photo

> 🧑‍🏫 Explain: people change photos to make them feel different, to make art, or to trick you.

## Look for clues
![A magnifying glass looking for clues]({{res:l6-magnifier.png}})

- snow in one part but not another
- something far too big or too small
- something in the wrong place

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. On the worksheet pupils SORT photos into real / changed and spot a clue. Even adults get this wrong sometimes.

## Always ask: is it real?
- not every photo you see is real
- look carefully for clues
- it is okay to be unsure

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do then plenary. TA: prompt pupils to point at the clue. End the unit by celebrating their best photos.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show your best photo and tell me one clue that a photo has been changed.

> 🧑‍🏫 End of unit. Note who can take a composed photo and spot a changed image.
