# What makes a good photograph?

## Today we are learning
- spot what is wrong with a photo
- say how to take a good photo
- take a well-composed photo
- improve a photo by taking it again

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: framing · subject · compose. Recap: portrait and landscape.

## Which photos need improving?
![A goat taken far too close]({{res:l3-a-goat-photo-taken-far-too-close-teach-computing.png}})
![A butterfly too far away to see]({{res:l3-a-butterfly-too-far-away-to-see-teach-computing.png}})

- too close — the subject is squashed
- too far — you cannot see the detail
- blurry — the camera moved

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. On the worksheet pupils SORT photos into good / needs improving. Keep it kind — there is no single right answer.

## Three things to check
- **Positioning** — is it clear what the photo is of?
- **Framing** — is the whole subject in the picture?
- **Detail** — are you close enough?

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. Use these three words every time. Likely error: cutting off part of the subject → fix-words "fit the whole thing in the frame."

## A good photo
![A clear, well-composed beach photo]({{res:l3-a-clear-well-composed-beach-photo-teach-computing.png}})

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Ask pupils to point out positioning, framing and detail in this photo.

## Your turn — take and review

- rate each one
- choose one to **take again** and make better

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. The Paper frame handout helps pupils choose portrait/landscape. TA: prompt the three checks, do not retake it for them.
