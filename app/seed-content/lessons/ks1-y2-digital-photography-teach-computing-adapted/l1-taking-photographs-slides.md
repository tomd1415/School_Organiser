# Taking photographs

## Today we are learning
- name devices that can take photographs
- hold a device to take a photograph
- take a photograph with a digital device
- say what I did to take my photograph

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: device · camera · photograph · capture · image · digital. This is the first lesson of the unit — set the routine.

## Photograph or drawing?
- a **photograph** is captured with a camera
- a **drawing** is made by hand
- both show things, but they are made in different ways

> 🧑‍🏫 Show a photo next to a drawing. Agree: photographs are taken with a camera. A tablet has a camera inside it.

## Can these take photos?
![A tablet]({{res:l1-a-tablet-teach-computing.png}})
![A printer]({{res:l1-a-printer-teach-computing.png}})

- a device with a **camera** can take photos
- a printer and a USB stick cannot
- a tablet, phone, laptop and camera can

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. On the worksheet pupils SORT devices into "can take photos / cannot". Likely error: a desktop computer usually needs a webcam plugged in.

## How to take a photo
![A photographer]({{res:l1-a-photographer-taking-a-photo-teach-computing.png}})

- **Hold** the device still
- **Look** at the screen
- **Press** the button

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do then we-do. Model the three steps. Likely error: not looking at the screen in the excitement → fix-words "look first, then press."

## Your turn — photo challenge
- take a photo of a building, a close-up, something far away, a selfie
- hold still · look · press
- always ask before photographing a person

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Hand out devices. TA: prompt the three steps, do not take the photo for them. Watch background — do not capture other people.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Tell me one thing you did to take your photo.

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils share their best photo and why they like it. Next lesson: portrait and landscape.
