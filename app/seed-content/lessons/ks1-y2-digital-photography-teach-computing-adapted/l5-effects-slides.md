# Effects

## Today we are learning
- see that a photo can be changed
- use a tool to change the colour
- save my edited photo with a clear name
- explain my choice

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: editing · filter. Recap: lighting and clear photos.

## The same photo, changed
![A dog with normal colours]({{res:l5-dog-original.jpg}})
![The same dog with a blue tint]({{res:l5-dog-blue.jpg}})
![The same dog with a red tint]({{res:l5-dog-red.jpg}})

- a **blue** tint feels cold
- a **red** tint feels warm
- it is the same photo — only the colour changed

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. On the worksheet pupils MATCH the colour effect to the feeling. Editing = changing a photo on a computer.

## The editing software
![The photo editor with a picture open]({{res:l5-editor-screenshot.png}})

- open the photo
- find the **Adjust** tools
- move a **slider** to change the colour

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. Model opening the dog photo and moving one colour slider. Keep it slow and predictable.

## Your turn — change the colour
- open your photo
- try the colour sliders
- make it feel cold, then warm

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do then you-do. TA: prompt one slider at a time. Likely error: dragging every slider at once → fix-words "one slider, then look."

## Save a copy
- choose **Save as a copy**
- type a **clear name** (e.g. "Dog black and white")
- the first photo is still safe

> 🧑‍🏫 Model saving a copy. Stress clear file names. Likely error: overwriting the original → fix-words "Save **as a copy**, not Save."

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show your edited photo and say what you changed and why.
