# Follow the breadcrumbs

## Today we are learning
- explain what a navigation path is
- describe why navigation paths are useful
- make multiple web pages and link them with hyperlinks

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: breadcrumb trail · navigation · hyperlink · subpage. Recap: last lesson we built a home page; today we add more pages and link them.

## Starter — breadcrumbs
![A site map showing how pages link]({{res:l5-site-map.png}})

- in Hansel and Gretel, breadcrumbs show the way back
- on a website, a breadcrumb trail shows where you have been

> 🧑‍🏫 Briefly recap the story. Ask why the breadcrumbs mattered. Link it to not getting lost on a website. Support pupils choose what a navigation path is.

## Where will you go?
![Exploring the zoo website]({{res:l5-zoo-website.png}})

- start on the home page
- click a link to a subpage, then go back
- that route is the navigation path

> 🧑‍🏫 Talk through a route live: home → Animals → cheetah → back → arctic fox. Pupils order a navigation path on the worksheet. Fix-words: "you go BACK to the page, then click the next link."

## Plan your website
- home page at the top
- subpages branch off it
- this plan is the site map

> 🧑‍🏫 Model a site map. Pupils plan their own subpages. Challenge: why plan the path first? TA: prompt one branch at a time.

## Subpages and hyperlinks
- a subpage is a page linked from another
- a hyperlink is what you click to move between pages

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the add-subpage and add-hyperlink videos. Pupils add subpages and link them. Show your work: screenshot the linked pages. Likely error: a link that goes nowhere — check each one works.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Tell me what a navigation path is.

> 🧑‍🏫 Listen for "the route you take through the pages." Movement break before next lesson.
