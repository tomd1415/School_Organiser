# Copyright or copyWRONG?

## Today we are learning
- say why I should use copyright-free images
- find copyright-free images
- describe what 'fair use' means
- use technology respectfully and responsibly

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: copyright · fair use · Creative Commons · copyright-free · credit. Recap: last lesson we planned our page; now we find images for it.

## Starter — where do pictures come from?
![The copyright symbol]({{res:l3-copyright.png}})

- you might search the internet for a picture
- but the person who made it owns it

> 🧑‍🏫 Build a class list of where pictures come from; keep it for the plenary. Likely belief: "everything online is free." We will see that is not true.

## Copyright and fair use
- copyright = the maker owns it; ask permission
- fair use = sometimes OK to use a small part, with credit
- be respectful: credit the people whose work you use

> 🧑‍🏫 Ask: "how would you feel if someone stole your work?" Link to being a respectful, responsible technology user. Support pupils choose what copyright means.

## Creative Commons — free to use
![Creative Commons licences]({{res:l3-cc-licences.png}})

- Creative Commons images are shared for others to use
- often you must credit (name) the maker
- choose "Creative Commons" in your image search

> 🧑‍🏫 Model choosing Creative Commons in the school-approved search (the "more/tools" menu). Pupils find and save a copyright-free image. TA: follow the school's online-safety steps if anything unsuitable appears.

## Copyright or copyWRONG?
- read each example
- decide: OK, or not OK?

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils sort the scenarios into OK / Not OK. The point is to debate the ethics. Fix-words: "even if it looks the same, still CHECK the licence." Challenge question covers Abdul's case.
