# Ordering commands

## Today we are learning
- choose a sound block to play notes
- explain that the order of notes changes the tune
- order sound blocks to play a tune
- play notes together to make a chord

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: sequence · order · note · chord. Same routine as every lesson. Headphones optional; sound is part of today's task.

## Two sound blocks
![Start sound blocks and play sound until done blocks]({{res:l4-sound-blocks.png}})

- play sound UNTIL DONE — one note, then the next (a tune)
- start sound — all notes at once (a chord)

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter (10 min). Play both for the class so they hear the difference. On the worksheet pupils choose which block makes a tune and which makes a chord.

## Order is the tune
![A tune made of play sound until done blocks]({{res:l4-tune-sequence.png}})

- each block plays one note, in order
- change the order and the tune changes
- same notes, different order = different tune

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do → we-do. The order of the notes IS the tune. Likely error: using "start sound" for a tune so all notes play at once. Fix-words: "use play sound UNTIL DONE so each note waits its turn."

## You-do — order a tune
- drag the sound blocks into order
- build it in Scratch
- listen — does it sound right?
- if not, check the order and run again

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do (about 23 min). Pupils order the blocks on the worksheet, then build and test. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Then screenshot.

## Make a chord
- swap to "start sound" blocks
- choose three notes
- they all start together = a chord

> 🧑‍🏫 Challenge step. A chord = several notes at once, so "start sound" is the right block. Let pupils hear the difference between their tune and their chord.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Play me your tune or your chord.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Note who can explain why a tune uses "until done" and a chord uses "start sound".
