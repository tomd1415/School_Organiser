# Environmental impact

## Today we are learning
- explain how technology harms the environment
- explain how technology can protect the environment
- explain why e-waste is harmful

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: environmental · disposal · pollution · e-waste · upgrade culture · data centre · preserver · destroyer.

## Starter — globalisation, both sides
Review the homework: technology connects the world, but what is the downside?

> 🧑‍🏫 Draw out a downside for each good effect (e.g. worldwide trade means more shipping and emissions). Links last lesson to this one.

## Technology as preserver
- renewable energy: solar, wind, hydro
- smart homes, electric cars, pollution sensors

> 🧑‍🏫 Start positive. Lord Broers' 2005 Reith Lectures argued technology can help save the planet. Pupils could research one preserver technology.

## Technology as destroyer — e-waste
![A skip full of old computers and electronic waste]({{res:l6-e-waste-pile-teach-computing.jpg}})

Old devices become e-waste — often shipped abroad and broken apart by hand.

> 🧑‍🏫 The recycling video (teacher-played hook) shows toxins like lead, cadmium and mercury harming recyclers' health. Ask pupils to note one harm.

## Why e-waste is harmful
- toxic metals leak into soil and water
- it harms the health of the people who recycle it
- rare materials are wasted

> 🧑‍🏫 Sort task and "tick all the reasons" on the worksheet. The order task traces an old phone's journey. Likely error: "e-waste makes phones cheaper" ticked → fix-words "that is not a HARM."

## Data centres and upgrade culture
- "the cloud" = huge data centres full of computers
- they use energy to run AND to stay cool
- buying a new phone every year (upgrade culture) makes more waste

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: why do data centres use so much energy? Stretch: how does upgrade culture add to the problem?
