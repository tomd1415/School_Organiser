## The Freedom of Information Act 2000
- any member of the public can ask a public body for recorded information
- the body can refuse if it costs too much, is vexatious, or repeats a request

> 🧑‍🏫 Important: the Act does NOT give you your own medical/credit records — that is a Data Protection subject access request. Worksheet has the "tick all the reasons to refuse" task.

## The Computer Misuse Act 1990
- it makes "hacking" — using a computer you are not allowed to — a crime
- punishment can be a fine, prison, or both

> 🧑‍🏫 Came from the Schifreen and Gold case. Stress: even just looking, with no damage, is still a crime (Level 1).

## The three levels
- **Level 1** — unauthorised access
- **Level 2** — unauthorised access, planning a further crime
- **Level 3** — unauthorised acts that damage or change things
