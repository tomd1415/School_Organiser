## Privacy vs security
- **privacy** — your right to keep information about yourself
- **security** — keeping everyone safe, which can mean collecting data
- the law tries to balance the two

> 🧑‍🏫 Two acts may be new: Telecommunications Act 1984 and Investigatory Powers Act 2016. Both let agencies collect data — both are controversial.

## The laws — who does what
- **Data Protection Act 2018** — how your data must be handled
- **Freedom of Information Act 2000** — public's right to information
- **Investigatory Powers Act 2016** — collecting data to stop crime
- **Computer Misuse Act 1990** — makes hacking a crime

> 🧑‍🏫 Matching task on the worksheet. Likely error: mixing DPA and FOIA → fix-words "DPA protects YOUR data; FOIA lets you see what PUBLIC bodies hold."

## Your data on social media
- apps collect a lot: messages, photos, location, what you tap
- "free" apps make money from your data

> 🧑‍🏫 The Guardian "all the data Facebook and Google have on you" is the hook (teacher-summarised). We would never let a stranger put a camera in our home — yet we carry trackers willingly.

## Social media and the environment
- **bad:** data centres, routers and devices all use energy and create e-waste
- **good:** online meetings replace some travel; campaigns spread fast

> 🧑‍🏫 Two sides again. This sets up Lesson 6 (environment). You-do: pupils give one bad and one good effect.
