# Cultural impacts

## Today we are learning
- explain what cultural impact means
- define downtime and the digital divide
- give positive and negative effects of mobile technology

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: cultural · downtime · digital divide · globalisation · mobile technology. Be sensitive — never compare pupils' own access or backgrounds.

## Starter — a paperless school
What would change if your school kept everything in the cloud and used no paper?

> 🧑‍🏫 Draw out both sides: greener and always up to date, BUT cost, data safety, and students with no device at home. Tick the things to think about on the worksheet.

## What is cultural impact?
- **culture** = "how we do things around here" — our ideas and habits
- **cultural impact** = how technology changes the way we live and interact

> 🧑‍🏫 Example: the internet changed how we research, shop and talk to friends. Keep it concrete.

## Globalisation
![Hands reaching towards planet Earth — a connected world]({{res:l4-globalisation-a-connected-world-teach-computing.jpg}})

Technology connects the whole world — trade, ideas and culture are shared.

> 🧑‍🏫 Globalisation = the world becoming more connected. Email, the internet and music tech are examples. This links to the homework idea.

## Downtime
- **downtime** = when an organisation loses some or all of its IT systems
- it costs money, lost sales and lost trust

> 🧑‍🏫 Causes: human error, hardware/software failure, or attacks. Ask: what happens to a shop's tills when the system is down?

## The digital divide
- the gap between people who **can** use technology and people who **cannot**
- affects older people, people on low incomes, remote areas, some disabled people

> 🧑‍🏫 Worked example (I do): name one group. We do: one way to help them. You do: tick the affected groups + write one way to reduce the divide. Likely error: "new phones" ticked — fix-words "the divide is about who is left OUT, not who has the newest device."

## Mobile technology — both sides
Drag the effects of mobile phones into "good for us" and "bad for us".

> 🧑‍🏫 Card-sort on the worksheet. Both sides are true — phones connect us but can become addictive. Stretch: who is responsible for reducing the harm (users? developers?).
