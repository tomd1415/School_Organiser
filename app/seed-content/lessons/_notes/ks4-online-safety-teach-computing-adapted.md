## SEND / safeguarding adaptation
This unit is safeguarding-adjacent (scams, illegal content, reputation attacks, privacy). All tasks kept
**strictly cohort-level** — no task asks a pupil to disclose their own accounts, history, passwords, or
personal experience. The original TCC "reflect on YOUR online activity / design your own data-theft attack /
ex-partner shares intimate messages" framings were **reframed**:
- L1 questionnaire ("rate yourself") → general threat sort + recognition (no self-disclosure).
- L2 reputation attackers → kept scammer/cyberbully/hacker; **dropped the ex-partner intimate-messages
  scenario** (safeguarding); added a "report, block, tell a trusted adult" cue on the slide + plan.
- L5 "design your own attack" → **spot-the-scam** multi-select + "what to do" order (defensive, not offensive).
- L7 illegal content kept **neutral/non-graphic** (hacking, copyright, threatening messages); slide tells
  pupils to move on / speak privately if a topic worries them.
- L8 country comparison kept about **systems, not naming/【criticising individuals**.
