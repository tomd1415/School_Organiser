# Answering questions

## Today we are learning
- say what data is
- choose the right table to answer a question
- think of questions data can answer
- spot data that is collected over time

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: data · table · heading · over time. Same routine as every lesson.

## Data around us
- DATA is information we collect
- the school takes the register every morning and afternoon
- this tells us who is in and who is away

> 🧑‍🏫 The register is data collected OVER TIME — twice a day, every day. This idea (data over time) is what the whole unit is about.

## Three tables
- Table A is about the weather
- Table B is from a fitness tracker
- Table C is football results
- the HEADINGS tell you what each table holds

> 🧑‍🏫 Point at the headings. "Which table would show the warmest day?" → A. We are not finding one day, we are choosing the right table. Some questions can't be answered by any table → X.

## Some questions have no answer here
- a table can only answer a question about its headings
- "what did you have for lunch?" — none of the tables can answer that

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: pupils pick any table. Fix-words: "check the headings first — if it's not a heading, the answer is X."

## Collecting over time
- some data is collected again and again, over time
- some data is collected just once
- your steps change all day → collected over time

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the card-sort on the worksheet. Support: do the first one together. Challenge: ask them to add their own example to each group.

## Your turn — count the cars

- watch the road video
- count ONE car colour
- make a tally as you watch

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Tell me one question your car tally could answer.
