# Answering my question

## Today we are learning
- find and review my collected data
- use my data to answer my question
- write a conclusion from my data
- say why a data logger is useful

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: review · analyse · conclusion · benefit. Same routine as every lesson.

## Reviewing your data
- open the data your logger collected
- check it is YOUR data
- look at the highest and lowest points

> 🧑‍🏫 Help pupils download or open their file. Remind them how to hide data they don't need and zoom in.

## Read the graph
![A light graph with two dips]({{res:l6-light-graph.png}})

- a dip down = it got darker / quieter / colder
- a peak up = the opposite
- the line tells a story over time

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the starter graph. Model reading a conclusion: "the light dipped twice, so the room got darker twice."

## Write your conclusion
- a CONCLUSION is what your data tells you
- answer your question using your numbers
- did it match your prediction?

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: pupils complete their report (question, prediction, what they found, conclusion). TA: prompt, do not write it for them.

## Why use a data logger?
- it collects data on its own
- it works when you are not there
- it collects the same way every time
- it might catch something you would miss

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the multi-select on the worksheet. Stress: a logger does NOT do your writing for you — that is the wrong option. Think-pair-share the benefits.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Tell me one benefit of a data logger.

> 🧑‍🏫 End-of-unit recap: question → choose sensor → log over time → analyse → conclusion. Celebrate the journey.
