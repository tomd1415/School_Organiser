# Sound file size calculation

## Today we are learning
- calculate the file size of a sound file
- describe how sample rate, duration, and resolution change the size and quality

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: sample rate · sample resolution · duration · bits · bytes. Hand out both worksheets.

## Starter — recap the terms
What is the sample rate? What is the sample resolution? What unit is the sample rate in?

> 🧑‍🏫 Answers: samples per second; bits per sample; hertz (Hz). These three terms are used all lesson.

## The formula  (I do)
- File size in bits = sample rate (Hz) × sample resolution (bits) × duration (seconds).
- Change minutes into seconds first: minutes × 60.

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep the formula on the board. Slow and plain. Watch for pupils forgetting to change minutes into seconds.

## Worked example  (we do)
2-second clip, 1,000 Hz, 4 bits:
- 1,000 × 4 × 2 = 8,000 bits

> 🧑‍🏫 Do this together. Then a we-do: a 2-minute (120 s) clip at 1,000 Hz, 4 bits → 480,000 bits.

## Your turn  (you do)
- Use the activity sheet. Change the time to seconds, then multiply the three numbers.

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: using minutes instead of seconds. Fix-words: "seconds first — minutes times sixty." TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Size and quality
- Higher sample rate, higher resolution, or longer recording → bigger file.
- Higher sample rate and resolution also → better quality.
- Sound metadata: artist, title, album, length.

> 🧑‍🏫 Each pupil says one thing that makes a sound file bigger.

Tick your "I can…". Tell me the sound file-size formula.
