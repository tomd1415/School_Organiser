# Measurements of storage

## Today we are learning
- name the units of storage from bit to petabyte
- compare the kibibyte, mebibyte and gibibyte to the kilobyte, megabyte and gigabyte
- convert between units of measurement

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: bit · nibble · byte · kilobyte · megabyte · gigabyte · terabyte · petabyte. Hand out both worksheets.

## Starter — order the units
How many bits in a byte? In a nibble? Put bit, nibble, byte, kilobyte, megabyte, gigabyte in order.

> 🧑‍🏫 Answers: 8 bits in a byte, 4 in a nibble. The order activity is on the starter sheet.

## The units  (I do)
- bit → nibble (4 bits) → byte (8 bits).
- Then each is 1,000 times the last: kilobyte → megabyte → gigabyte → terabyte → petabyte.

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep the table on the board. A class "silly sentence" can help remember the order — agree one and reuse it.

## Converting  (we do)
- To go up a unit, multiply. To go down a unit, divide.
- 2 GB = 2 × 1,000 = 2,000 MB = 2,000,000 kB.

> 🧑‍🏫 Do 2 GB → MB together. Likely error: jumping two units in one step. Fix-words: "one unit at a time, times a thousand."

## Putting them in order  (you do)
- To compare different units, change them all to the same unit (e.g. bits) first.
- 3 nibbles = 12 bits, 14 bits, 2 bytes = 16 bits, 1 MB is huge.

> 🧑‍🏫 Challenge does the A/B/C/D ordering. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## The 'bibyte' units
- kibibyte, mebibyte, gibibyte go up in 1,024s, not 1,000s.
- This is why a "1 TB" drive shows a little less on a computer.

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep this brief — it is the trickiest idea. Plain line: "computers count in 1,024s, shops count in 1,000s."

## I can…
Tick your "I can…". Tell me how many megabytes are in a gigabyte.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who converted one unit at a time without a reminder.
