# What is representation

## Today we are learning
- give examples of representation
- computers represent all data and instructions in binary
- binary relates to two-state on/off signals
- how many things a group of switches can represent

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: data · instructions · binary · coding system · transistor. Same routine every lesson. This is the first lesson of the unit — keep it calm and concrete.

## Starter — one thing standing for another
- A symbol can stand for a word or an idea.
- Writing let messages travel far and last a long time.
- A representation only works if everyone agrees what it means.

> 🧑‍🏫 Think–write–pair–share. No writing needed for Support — they tick. Big idea: a representation is one thing standing for another.

## Data and instructions
- **Data** = numbers or text that, on their own, have no meaning (e.g. 200).
- **Instructions** = how to do a task (e.g. "add", "preheat the oven").
- A calculator holds the **data** and follows the **instructions**.

![A calculator: the numbers are data, the operations are instructions]({{res:l1-calculator-data-and-instructions-teach-computing.png}})

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do → we-do. Sort the recipe parts on the worksheet (card-sort). Likely mix-up: numbers that tell you "how much" feel like instructions — fix-words: "a number on its own is data; a verb like mix or bake is an instruction."

## Two states: on and off
- A computer is full of tiny switches called **transistors**.
- Each one is **on (1)** or **off (0)** — two states.
- That is **binary**: everything is built from 1s and 0s.

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Say "1 means on, 0 means off." Strong visual: a light switch. No flashing — just point to on/off.

## More switches, more things  (you do)
- 1 switch → 2 things
- 2 switches → 4 things (00, 01, 10, 11)
- 3 switches → 8 things
- The pattern is **2 to the power of** the number of switches.

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do on the worksheet. Support: tick on/off. Core: write the 4 combinations for 2 switches. Challenge: 10 switches → 2^10 = 1024. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Screenshot the working.
