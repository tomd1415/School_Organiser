# Battle Boats — the project

## Today we are learning
- explain the rules of Battle Boats
- describe what success criteria are
- break the project into clear success criteria
- spot how an old program could help

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: success criteria · decomposition. This is lesson 1 of the Battle Boats project — the unit's summative assessment, spread over the next lessons. Calm, steady pace.

## Starter — play the game
- Two 8×8 grids each: a **fleet grid** (your boats `B`) and a **target tracker** (`H` hit, `M` miss).
- Take turns firing at a coordinate. Sink all 5 enemy boats to win.

> 🧑‍🏫 Print one A0 paper sheet per pair. Play one round unplugged so the rules are concrete before coding. If a pair finishes, ask: what steps would the program need? No screens for this part.

## What are success criteria?  (I do)
- A clear, **testable** list of everything the program must do.
- One statement per line, broken down as far as you can (**decomposition**).
- Example: "the program lets the player place exactly 5 boats."

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. Contrast a good criterion ("marks a hit with H") with a vague one ("make it good"). Card-sort good vs vague. Everyone aims for up to Task 7; explorer tasks if there is time.

## The tasks  (we do)
1. Menu → 2. Player places 5 boats → 3. Computer places its boats → 4. Show the target tracker → 5. Player's turn (hit/miss) → 6. Computer's turn → 7. Play until a winner.

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Order the steps of a turn together. This is the spine of the success criteria. Don't rush — thinking time, circulate to check understanding.

## Your turn  (you do)
- Core: write the success criteria for Tasks 1–7, broken down.
- Challenge: list parts of your noughts and crosses game you could reuse.

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. It is fine — encouraged — to reuse old code (the 2D-grid noughts and crosses is a great start). TA: prompt, don't write criteria for them. Screenshot the sheet at the end.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me your success criteria.
