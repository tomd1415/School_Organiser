## A picture is a mosaic
A mosaic is a picture made of small coloured squares. A digital picture is the same — the squares are called **pixels**.

![A street-art mosaic made of square tiles]({{res:l1-binary-mosaic.jpg}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Unplugged: pairs build a small grid picture, then write 1 bit (black/white) per pixel and swap. Pixels sit in rows and columns.

## Zoom in — you see the pixels
Zoom right in on any photo and the pixels appear.

![A photo zoomed in until the pixels show]({{res:l1-pixels-zoom-astronaut.jpg}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Each pixel is one small square of colour. The whole image is just pixels in a grid.

## Resolution and colour depth
- **resolution** = how many pixels (width × height)
- **colour depth** = how many bits are used for each pixel's colour

![Concept map: image → bitmap → pixels → resolution and colour depth]({{res:l1-bitmap-concept-map.jpg}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep the two words apart: resolution is *how many* pixels; colour depth is *how many bits per* pixel.

## Count the bits  (I do)
A picture is 9 pixels wide and 10 pixels tall. Each pixel uses 3 bits.
- pixels = 9 × 10 = 90
- bits = 90 × 3 = **270 bits**

> 🧑‍🏫 Worked example first. Rule: pixels × bits per pixel = total bits. Likely error: multiplying by the number of colours instead of the colour depth → "use bits per pixel, not the number of colours."
