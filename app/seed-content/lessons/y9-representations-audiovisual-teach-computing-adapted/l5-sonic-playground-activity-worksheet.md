## What we are learning
Today I will work out how big a sound file is, and edit sound with software.

## Build the formula — put the steps in order
Drag the steps into the right order to find the size of a sound.

```order
Count how many samples are taken each second (sampling rate)
Multiply by the bits used for each sample (sample size)
Multiply by how many seconds the sound lasts (duration)
Multiply by the number of channels
```

![The Audacity sound editor]({{res:l5-audacity-logo.png}})

## Worked example
Rule: **sampling rate × sample size × duration × channels = size in bits**.

1 second of sound, 8000 samples per second, 1 byte (8 bits) per sample, 1 channel:
- 8000 × 1 byte × 1 second = **8000 bytes** = 8 kilobytes
