# Fake news

## Today we are learning
- say what fake news is
- tell the difference between fact and opinion
- spot signs that news might be fake
- name ways to check a story

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: fake news · disinformation · fact · opinion · source · reverse image search. Same routine.

## What is fake news?
- information spread ON PURPOSE to mislead
- not the same as an honest mistake
- not just news you disagree with

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the starter single choice. Stress "on purpose" — that is the key word. Keep examples neutral and non-political.

## Fact or opinion?
- a FACT can be checked
- an OPINION is what someone thinks
- fake news often hides opinion as fact

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the starter card sort. Read each card aloud first for the Support group. Likely error: calling a strong opinion a fact — fix-words: "can I check it? Then it's a fact."

## Signs news might be fake
- a shocking headline that wants a fast share
- no author or date
- an odd website name

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the spot-the-fake multi-select on the activity worksheet. Go through each sign with an example.

## How to check a story
1. read past the headline
2. check the author and date
3. look on trusted sites
4. reverse image search the photo

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the order task. Model one check live if you have a device. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.
