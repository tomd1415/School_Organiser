# An introduction to online safety

## Today we are learning
- name some online safety threats
- sort threats into data theft and data misuse
- match online safety words to their meaning
- say which threats are common

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Same calm routine as every lesson. Vocabulary on the board: threat · phishing scam · malware · fake news · big data · targeted advertising. This is a discussion topic — keep it general, never ask anyone to share something personal.

## Starter — what is a threat?
- a THREAT is something online that could harm you, your data, or your device
- not everything online is a threat

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the starter worksheet single choice. Likely error: thinking a password manager or update is a threat — fix-words: "a threat tries to HARM you; updates and password tools PROTECT you."

## The online safety words
- phishing scam — a fake message that tricks you
- malware — harmful software
- fake news — made-up news
- big data — huge data sets companies study

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep definitions short and literal. This matches the matching task on the starter worksheet. Point to each word on the board as you say it.

## Sort the threats — theft or misuse?
- DATA THEFT = someone tries to take your data (phishing, malware, hacking)
- DATA MISUSE = data already collected is used in a way you may not like (adverts, filter bubble, fake news)

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the card sort on the activity worksheet. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Support pairs read each card aloud first.

## Which threats are common?
- some threats happen every day (phishing, adverts)
- some are rarer
- all of them are worth knowing about

> 🧑‍🏫 Reassure the class: knowing about threats keeps you safe — it is not meant to scare anyone. Use the challenge multi-select.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Tell me one online safety threat you can name.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can name and sort threats. This sets the base for the whole unit.
