# Big data

## Today we are learning
- say what big data is
- name the three Vs (volume, variety, velocity)
- say why companies collect data
- give a good and a bad point about big data

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: big data · data analytics · the three Vs · targeted advertising. Same routine.

## What is big data?
- very LARGE sets of data
- studied to find PATTERNS about people
- companies use it to learn about their customers

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the starter single choice. Keep the numbers concrete: a big company can hold billions of records.

## What data is collected?
- obvious: name, email, what you bought
- less obvious: pages you viewed, how long you stayed, what you clicked

> 🧑‍🏫 The "less obvious" data usually surprises pupils. Matches the starter card sort. Likely error: only listing obvious data — fix-words: "websites also record what you LOOK at, not just what you buy."

## The three Vs
- VOLUME — a huge amount of data
- VARIETY — many different types
- VELOCITY — it arrives very fast

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the matching task on the activity worksheet. Say each V slowly and give one short example.

## Good and bad
- helpful: better suggestions, spotting fraud
- harmful: collected without you knowing, sold on, one-sided content

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the card sort and the slider. There is no single right answer — the aim is a balanced opinion. TA: prompt quieter pupils to pick a side and give one reason.
