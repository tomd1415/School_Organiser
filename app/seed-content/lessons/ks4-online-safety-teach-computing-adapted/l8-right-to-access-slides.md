# Right to access

## Today we are learning
- say what censorship is
- compare internet rules in different countries
- name privacy-enhancing technologies
- give an opinion on the right to access

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: censorship · VPN · Tor · encryption · privacy-enhancing technologies. Keep the country comparison factual and balanced.

## What is censorship?
- blocking or hiding content
- decided by someone in charge (a country, a school)
- it can be sensible OR unfair

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the starter single choice and card sort. Blocking malware = sensible; blocking the news = a concern. Let pupils see both sides.

## Rules around the world
- the UK blocks some illegal content
- some countries block much more, such as news and social media
- some countries block almost nothing

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep it simple and neutral: UK, and "some countries block more / less". Avoid singling out individuals; this is about systems, not people.

## Privacy-enhancing technologies
![The Tor Browser start page]({{res:l8-tor-browser.png}})

- encryption — scrambles data into code
- VPN — hides your location
- Tor — a browser that hides who you are

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the matching task. Stress: these tools have GOOD and RISKY uses. Likely error: thinking they are only for criminals — fix-words: "they protect ordinary people's privacy too."

## Should they be allowed?
- they protect privacy and free speech
- they can also help people break the law
- it is a balance

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the challenge and the slider. No right answer; coach a balanced opinion. TA: prompt one point each way.
