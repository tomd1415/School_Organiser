# Illegal content

## Today we are learning
- say why some online content is harmful
- name some UK laws about online content
- match a law to the content it bans
- say why policing the internet is hard

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: illegal content · online crime · Computer Misuse Act · Copyright Designs and Patents Act · Malicious Communications Act. Keep examples neutral and non-graphic. If anyone is worried by a topic, move on and speak to them privately.

## Legal or illegal?
- some online actions break the law
- many actions are perfectly fine
- "it was online" is not an excuse

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the starter card sort. Keep it concrete and calm: hacking, sharing films without permission, threatening messages.

## UK laws — there are many
- Computer Misuse Act (1990) — hacking and malware
- Copyright Designs and Patents Act (1988) — using others' work
- Malicious Communications Act (1988) — threatening messages

> 🧑‍🏫 The big idea: the UK uses MANY laws, not one. This matches the matching task. Likely error: thinking there is one "internet law" — fix-words: "lots of laws, each for a different crime."

## Who is responsible?
- the person who made or shared the content
- sometimes the platform that hosts it
- both can be involved

> 🧑‍🏫 Brief discussion only — no need to resolve it. Keep it cohort-level.

## Why is it hard to police?
- a huge amount of content
- it can come from anywhere in the world
- some of it is encrypted

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the challenge multi-select. TA: prompt one reason each. Remind the class how to report content (in-app report, tell a trusted adult).
