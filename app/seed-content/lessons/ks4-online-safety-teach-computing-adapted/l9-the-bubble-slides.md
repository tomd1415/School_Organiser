## What is a filter bubble?
- you mostly see content you already agree with
- also called an echo chamber
- it builds from the data collected on you

> 🧑‍🏫 Link back to Lesson 3 (big data). Keep the definition short. Matches the core fill-the-gaps.

## How a bubble forms
1. a website collects what you click
2. it learns what you like
3. it shows more of the same
4. you see fewer different views

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the order task. Step through it slowly. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Why bubbles can be harmful
- fewer different opinions
- harder to spot fake news
- you may think everyone agrees with you

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the multi-select and the slider. To "burst" a bubble: follow a range of sources, check stories elsewhere. Coach a balanced view.
