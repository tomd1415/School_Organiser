# Protecting myself online

## Today we are learning
- name the main online safety threats from this unit
- match a situation to a safety tip
- choose safe actions online
- show what I have learned in the quiz

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. This is the last lesson — recap the whole unit calmly. Vocabulary: threat · phishing · fake news · filter bubble · privacy · safe action. Reassure: the internet is useful; the aim is good habits, not fear.

## Recap — the threats
- phishing, malware, hacking (data theft)
- big data, targeted ads, filter bubbles (data use)
- fake news and illegal content

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the starter matching and multi-select. Let pupils call out threats they remember. Praise recall.

## Match the tip to the situation
- unexpected link → do not click, check first
- new account → strong, different password
- shocking headline → check a trusted source

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the matching task on the activity worksheet. Keep tips short and doable.

## Safe or unsafe?
- safe: strong passwords, privacy settings, reporting
- unsafe: sharing passwords, clicking unknown links, oversharing

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the card sort and the order task. The strongest habit: STOP and check. A trusted adult is always a safe step.
