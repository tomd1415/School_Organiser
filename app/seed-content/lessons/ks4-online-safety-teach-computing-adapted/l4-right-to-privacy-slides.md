# Right to privacy

## Today we are learning
- sort information by how private it should be
- name UK privacy laws
- match a privacy law to what it does
- give an opinion on privacy

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: right to privacy · Human Rights Act · Data Protection Act · Investigatory Powers Act · terms and conditions. Keep it general, not personal.

## Starter — how private?
![Levels of privacy diagram]({{res:l4-levels-of-privacy.png}})

- some information should ALWAYS be private (passwords, bank details)
- for other information, it is a personal choice

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the diagram: completely private → close friends → family → companies → completely public. Stress: passwords are always in the centre. Matches the starter card sort.

## Privacy laws in the UK
- Human Rights Act (1998) — right to a private life
- Data Protection Act (2018) — controls how data is used
- Investigatory Powers Act (2016) — when the state can watch citizens

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep each law to one short line. This matches the matching task. Likely error: mixing up the laws — fix-words: "Data Protection = how DATA is used."

## Terms and conditions
- a contract you agree to for a service
- you often give up some rights in return
- most people do not read them

> 🧑‍🏫 Ask: is it a fair trade? No right answer — invite a balanced view.

## Is privacy always good?
- strong privacy protects you
- but it can make crimes harder to investigate
- it is a balance

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the challenge and the slider. Let pupils land on their own opinion; do not lead them. TA: prompt one reason each way.
