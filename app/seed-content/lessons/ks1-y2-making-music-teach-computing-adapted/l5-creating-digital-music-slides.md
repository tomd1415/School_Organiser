# Creating digital music

## Today we are learning
- choose an animal
- make a rhythm for my animal
- make my animal's rhythm on the computer
- add a pattern of notes to my rhythm

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: create · rhythm · pattern · notes · tempo · animal. Same routine as every lesson.

## An elephant walking
![An elephant walking]({{res:l5-an-elephant-walking-teach-computing.jpg}})

- big, slow and heavy
- a slow, heavy rhythm: BOOM … BOOM … BOOM
- the way it moves gives us a rhythm

> 🧑‍🏫 Optional: one volunteer walks slowly like an elephant while the class taps a slow beat. The starter worksheet asks about elephant and mouse rhythms.

## Choose your animal
![A crocodile]({{res:l5-a-crocodile-teach-computing.png}}) ![A bird]({{res:l5-a-bird-teach-computing.png}})

- pick an animal you like
- a mouse is fast and light
- an elephant is slow and heavy

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils choose an animal. Support: sort animals into fast and slow on the worksheet. Keep choices small (a few pictures) to avoid overload.

## Tap your animal's rhythm
- think how your animal moves
- tap the rhythm quietly with your hand
- keep it short and repeat it

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Tap quietly on the table. Likely error: the rhythm is too long → fix-words "keep it short, then repeat it."

## Make the rhythm in Song Maker
- use the bottom row for the rhythm
- use no more than three notes
- make it repeat

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do then you-do. Model a short repeating rhythm at the bottom of Song Maker. Three notes is plenty.

## Add a pattern of notes
- add notes on the top part
- again, no more than three
- listen as you go

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Pupils add a short note pattern on top of their rhythm, then screenshot onto the worksheet. Challenge: explain how it sounds like their animal. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Save your work
- click Save, then Copy Link
- paste the link next to your name
- you need it next lesson

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Support pupils to save the link. Pupils self-tick the four "I can…". Movement break before the next task.
