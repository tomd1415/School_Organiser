# How music can be used

## Today we are learning
- connect pictures with sounds
- use a computer to change the pitch
- make high and low sounds
- make music about an idea

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: pitch · high · low · idea · Neptune. Same routine as every lesson.

## Neptune
![Neptune — the blue planet]({{res:l3-neptune-the-blue-planet-teach-computing.png}})

- Holst wrote music about Neptune
- it sounds mysterious and far away
- music can give us an idea

> 🧑‍🏫 Play about one minute of Neptune (teacher-played audio). Ask what they picture. Pupils answer on the starter worksheet.

## Drawing music
![A child's drawing of music]({{res:l3-a-child-s-drawing-of-music-teach-computing.jpg}})

- some children drew what they heard
- spiky shapes for loud, high sounds
- soft lines for quiet, low sounds

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the example. This is one way to connect a picture with a sound. Optional: pupils draw what Neptune sounds like.

## High and low (pitch)
- pitch means how high or low a sound is
- draw near the top → high sound
- draw near the bottom → low sound

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. Pitch is the key word today. The activity worksheet asks pupils to sort high and low sounds. Support: sort the sounds. Likely error: pupils mix up top/bottom → fix-words "high goes high, low goes low."

## Make sounds in Chrome Music Lab
![The Chrome Music Lab tools]({{res:l3-the-chrome-music-lab-tools-teach-computing.png}})

- open the Kandinsky tool
- draw lines and shapes to make sounds
- try the top for high, the bottom for low

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Model drawing a line near the top, then near the bottom. Let pupils experiment. Keep the volume low.

## Make music about an idea
![A rocket in space]({{res:l3-a-rocket-in-space-teach-computing.png}})

- choose a space thing (a rocket, a star, a comet)
- make music that fits it
- high and low sounds together

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Pupils pick a space object and make music for it, then screenshot onto the worksheet. Challenge: explain why they chose high or low. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Plenary: self-tick the four "I can…". Movement break.
