# Rhythms and patterns

## Today we are learning
- make a pattern
- play a rhythm from a pattern
- make a rhythm on the computer
- say that people make and play music

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: pattern · rhythm · pulse · beat. Same routine as every lesson.

## Recap
- last lesson we listened to music
- some music was loud, some was quiet
- music can make us feel things

> 🧑‍🏫 Quick recap only. Ask one or two pupils for a describing word from last lesson.

## A pattern
- a pattern repeats
- 🔴 🟡 🔴 🟡 🔴 🟡
- two colours, over and over

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the colour pattern. Ask what comes next. Pupils answer this on the starter worksheet. Use counters: one colour = one instrument.

## A rhythm
![Hands clapping a rhythm]({{res:l2-hands-clapping-a-rhythm-teach-computing.png}})

- a rhythm is a pattern of sounds
- people clap and tap rhythms
- music is made by people

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Clap a short rhythm together (clap, clap, rest, clap). Keep it quiet and steady. Pupils who do not want to clap can tap the table.

## Make a rhythm on the computer
![The Rhythm tool in Chrome Music Lab]({{res:l2-the-rhythm-tool-in-chrome-music-lab-teach-computing.png}})

- tap the dots to make a pattern
- press play to hear it
- change it and listen again

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do then you-do. Model tapping a few dots and pressing play. Likely error: too many dots at once → fix-words "start small, add one dot, then listen." Support: label the play button and dots on the worksheet. Challenge: explain how to fix a rhythm. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Computer or instrument?
- a drum is an instrument
- a computer can make a rhythm too
- people use both to make music

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Pupils self-tick the four "I can…" and screenshot their rhythm. Movement break before the next task.
