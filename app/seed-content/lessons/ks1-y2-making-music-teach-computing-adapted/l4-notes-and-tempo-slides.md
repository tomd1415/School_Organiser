# Notes and tempo

## Today we are learning
- say that music is a sequence of notes
- make a pattern of notes on the computer
- change the instrument
- change the tempo

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: note · pattern · sequence · instrument · tempo. Same routine as every lesson.

## Recap
- last lesson we made high and low sounds
- a rhythm is a pattern of sounds
- today we make a pattern of notes

> 🧑‍🏫 Quick recap. Remind pupils what a pattern is.

## A note
- in Song Maker each coloured block is a note
- music is a line of notes, one after another
- this is called a sequence

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. "Note" and "sequence" are the key words. Pupils answer "what is a coloured block?" on the starter worksheet.

## Song Maker
![The Chrome Music Lab tools]({{res:l4-the-chrome-music-lab-tools-teach-computing.png}})

- open Song Maker (the smiley face)
- click squares to add notes
- press play to hear them

> 🧑‍🏫 Model adding three notes and pressing play. Likely error: pupils add too many notes at once → fix-words "add one, then listen." Show how to remove a note by clicking it again. The worksheet asks pupils to order these steps.

## Change the instrument
- click the instrument button
- the same notes sound different
- try a few instruments

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Change the instrument and replay the same notes. Ask which they like best.

## Change the tempo
- tempo means how fast or slow
- drag the tempo slider
- fast or slow — your choice

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the tempo slider. Keep it slow so it stays low-arousal. The worksheet asks pupils to tick what they can change (notes, instrument, tempo).

## Save your work
- click Save, then Copy Link
- paste the link next to your name
- you will need it next lesson

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do then plenary. Saving is tricky — support pupils to copy and paste the link. Pupils self-tick the four "I can…". Movement break before the next task.
