# How music makes us feel

## Today we are learning
- hear the differences between two pieces of music
- describe music using words
- say how the music makes me feel
- say what I like and do not like

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: music · loud · quiet · fast · slow · feelings · Mars · Venus. Same routine as every lesson. No flashing or sudden sound — tell pupils before you press play.

## The Planets
![The planets in space]({{res:l1-solar-system.png}})

- Gustav Holst wrote music about the planets
- each planet has its own piece of music

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep it short. The starter worksheet shows this picture too. Some pupils may want headphones or to sit further from the speaker — offer this before playing audio.

## Listen — Mars
![Mars — the red planet]({{res:l1-mars.jpg}})

- loud and fast
- a marching beat
- sounds like a battle

> 🧑‍🏫 Play about one minute of Mars (teacher-played audio). Warn pupils it gets loud. Ask: loud or quiet? Pupils answer on the starter worksheet.

## Listen — Venus
![Venus — the orange planet]({{res:l1-venus.jpg}})

- quiet and slow
- gentle and floaty
- sounds calm and peaceful

> 🧑‍🏫 Play about one minute of Venus (teacher-played audio). Ask: how is it different from Mars? Likely error: pupils say "good/bad" not describing words → fix-words "tell me loud or quiet, fast or slow."

## Describing words
- loud · quiet
- fast · slow
- marching · gentle
- scary · calm

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Model sorting two words onto Mars and Venus. The activity worksheet asks pupils to sort the rest. Support: sort the words. Challenge: say which they liked best and why. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Computers can make music
![Coloured sound bars on a computer]({{res:l1-spectrogram.png}})

- you can also make music on a computer
- we will do this in the next lessons

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Pupils self-tick the four "I can…". Each pupil shares one describing word. Movement break before the next task.
