# How do digital devices help us?

## Today we are learning
- name jobs I can do on a digital device
- make art on a computer
- say what is the SAME as using paper
- say what is DIFFERENT from using paper

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: program · digital · non-digital. Same routine as every lesson.

## Digital devices help us do jobs
- write a story · play a game · paint a picture · send a message
- a **program** is the set of instructions that does the job

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do: name a device, ask what jobs it can do. Starter `multi-select` checks they know real jobs (not "eat an apple"). Keep it concrete.

## Two ways to paint
![A picture painted on paper]({{res:l3-a-painting-non-digital-art-teach-computing.png}})

- **digital** = a paint program on a computer
- **non-digital** = paper, pencil and paint

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the painting. We will make a picture BOTH ways and compare. If time is short, paint digitally and compare to paper they have used before.

## The paint program tools
- **brush** = draw a line
- **fill** = colour a whole shape
- **undo** = take back a mistake

> 🧑‍🏫 Model brush, fill and undo on the board. Likely error: pressing many buttons fast — fix-words "one tool at a time." TA: prompt the tool name, do not do it for them.

## Make your picture
![Paper and a pencil]({{res:l3-paper-and-pencil-non-digital-tools-teach-computing.png}})

- make your picture on the computer
- use brush, fill and undo
- take a screenshot for show-your-work

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: calm, one tool at a time. Low floor: a simple shape filled with colour. High ceiling: a detailed scene. No flashing brushes/effects.

## Same and different
- **same:** you choose colours, you make a picture
- **different:** a computer has undo; paper can smudge and dry

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do: build the same/different lists together. Core pupils write one of each. Ask which they preferred and why.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me your picture and say which way you preferred.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can give a real same/different point. Next lesson: how am I connected — networks.
