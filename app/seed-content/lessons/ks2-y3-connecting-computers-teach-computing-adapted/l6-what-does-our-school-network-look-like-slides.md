# What does our school network look like?

## Today we are learning
- name the parts of our school network
- say how the parts are joined
- find networked devices around school
- say why a network is useful

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: network cable · network socket. Same routine as every lesson. Plan the tour route in advance; invite the IT technician if you can.

## Remember our three devices
- switch → passes messages
- server → stores files
- wireless access point → joins devices with Wi-Fi

> 🧑‍🏫 Quick recall, pictures on the starter worksheet. Then add the new parts that JOIN everything together.

## Cables and sockets
![A network cable]({{res:l6-a-network-cable-teach-computing.png}})

- a **network cable** carries information between devices
- it plugs into a **network socket** in the wall

> 🧑‍🏫 Show a real cable and socket if you can. Wireless devices still reach a wireless access point that is joined by a cable.

## Networked devices — the printer
![A printer]({{res:l6-a-networked-printer-teach-computing.png}})

- a printer is on the network too
- many computers can send work to ONE printer

> 🧑‍🏫 Good real example of sharing: one printer, many users. Pupils will look for the printer on the tour.

## Wired or wireless?
- some parts join with a CABLE (computer, server, printer)
- some parts join with WI-FI (tablet, laptop)
- sort them on the activity worksheet

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do one of each, then pupils sort. Likely error: thinking a tablet uses a cable — fix-words "a tablet has no cable, it uses Wi-Fi." TA: prompt, do not sort it for them.

## Network tour
- walk quietly around school
- find: computers, printer, wireless access points, cables, sockets, switch, server
- take a photo of one part

> 🧑‍🏫 Calm, structured tour with a clear route. Give pupils the list of parts to spot. Photo goes in show-your-work. Movement break is part of the routine.

## Why is a network useful?
- share files with others
- use the same printer
- save your work on the server

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary discussion. Note: a computer does not need a network to reach the internet — keep that simple for now; the internet is the focus in Year 4.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Tell me one part of our school network and what it does.

> 🧑‍🏫 Check pupils can name a real network part they saw. End of unit — recap input/process/output and networks.
