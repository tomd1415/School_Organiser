## The project (the scenario)
You will build a Battle Boats game where the **player** plays against the **computer**. Over the next lessons you will design it, code it, test it, and evaluate it. Everyone aims to complete **up to Task 7**.

## A turn — put the steps in order
Drag the steps of the player's turn into the right order.

```order
Show a menu (new game, resume, instructions, quit)
Let the player place their 5 boats on the fleet grid
The computer secretly places its 5 boats
Player fires at a coordinate on the target tracker
Mark a hit (H) or a miss (M)
The computer takes its turn
Repeat until one fleet is sunk, then announce the winner
```

## Good or vague success criterion? — sort the cards
A success criterion is a clear, testable statement of one thing the program must do.

```sort
Good (clear and testable): the program lets the player place exactly 5 boats, the program marks a hit with H, the program stops the player firing at the same square twice
Vague (not testable): make it good, the game should be fun, do the battleships
```
