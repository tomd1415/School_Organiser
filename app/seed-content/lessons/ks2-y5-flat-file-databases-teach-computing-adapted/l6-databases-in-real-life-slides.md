# Databases in real life

## Today we are learning
- name fields in a real-world database
- search and filter to answer a question
- sort results to find the best flight
- present my findings to others

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: real-world database · field · search · filter · sort · present. Same routine as every lesson.

## Databases are everywhere
![An airport departure board]({{res:l6-departure-board.png}})

- shops, films and flights all use databases
- a flight database holds millions of records
- far too many to sort by hand

> 🧑‍🏫 Real-life hook: the departure board is a view of a flight database. Ask what data a flight database would store (times, airline, price).

## Fields on a flight
- departure airport, destination airport
- departure time, arrival time, date
- airline, how long, price

> 🧑‍🏫 Show a flight record. Ask for FIELD names, not values. These are the same idea as the database fields all unit.

## Search the flight database
![A flight search website]({{res:l6-expedia-search.png}})

- type "flying from" and "destination"
- choose the dates
- press search

> 🧑‍🏫 You are travel agents. Live-demo Manchester to Paris first, then they do Birmingham to New York. TA: prompt, do not type it for them.

## Sort and filter the results
![A list of flights with filters and a price sort]({{res:l6-flight-results.png}})

- SORT by price to find the cheapest
- FILTER by Stops to find direct flights
- FILTER by departure time for morning flights

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: reading the first flight as "the best" → fix-words "sort or filter FIRST, then read the top." Keep it calm and low-arousal.

## Present your flight
- say which flight you chose
- say why (price, time, stops)
- show your screenshot

> 🧑‍🏫 Small groups present one chosen flight. Keep it short and supportive. Self-tick the four "I can…". End-of-unit: recap data → record/field → search/sort → chart → real-world database.
