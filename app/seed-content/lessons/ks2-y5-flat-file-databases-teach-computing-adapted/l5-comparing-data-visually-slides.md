# Comparing data visually

## Today we are learning
- choose a chart that answers a question
- make a chart from a database
- say why a computer is good at making charts

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: chart · bar chart · pie chart · axis · filter · compare. Same routine as every lesson.

## Why use a chart?
- a chart turns data into a picture
- a picture is quick to compare
- we use charts in maths and science too

> 🧑‍🏫 Think-pair-share where they have seen a chart. Land on: charts make data quick to read and compare.

## Is the chart useful?
![Two charts of the same country data]({{res:l5-which-chart.png}})

- a chart must help answer the question
- Chart 1 (bars) shows the biggest area clearly
- a chart with no useful numbers does not help

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do: ask "which chart answers 'largest area' best?" Land on the bar chart. A chart that just counts each country once tells us nothing.

## Choose the right chart
- BAR chart — compare amounts side by side
- PIE chart — show parts of a whole
- match the chart to the question

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the matching task on the worksheet. Bar for comparing many; pie for parts of one whole.
