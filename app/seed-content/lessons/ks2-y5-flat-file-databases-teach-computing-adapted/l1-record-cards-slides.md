# Record cards

## Today we are learning
- say what data, a record and a field are
- fill in a record card
- sort and group cards to answer a question

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: data · database · record · field · sort · group. Same routine as every lesson.

## What is data?
- DATA is facts — words and numbers
- a DATABASE is a tidy store of data
- a database lets us find data again quickly

> 🧑‍🏫 Split the word: data + base. Ask where they have seen data (registers, scores, maths). Land on: a database keeps data tidy so we can find it.

## A record card
![One record from an animal database]({{res:l1-minibeast-card.png}})

- one whole card = a RECORD
- each box of facts = a FIELD
- the animal name goes in the middle

> 🧑‍🏫 Point: the whole card is one record; "How many legs", "Does it have wings" are fields. Misconception: "a field is the whole card" — fix-words "a field is ONE fact, a record is ALL the facts."

## Make your cards
- choose 8 animals
- write the name in the middle
- tick the fields that are true

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do one card on the board (e.g. bat: can fly ✓). They make 8. Support: use the scaffolded animal facts sheet. Keep them — we use them again in Lesson 3.

## Sort your cards
- mix the cards up
- put the names in A–Z order
- time it with a partner

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do: sort your own set and make ONE deliberate mistake. Ask "what went wrong?" Likely error: looking at colour not the name → fix-words "sort by the NAME."

## Group to answer a question
- "Which animals can fly?"
- pull out only the cards with that tick
- that is a GROUP

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: pose a question, they group the cards. TA: prompt, do not sort it for them. Ask how they knew — praise using the ticks.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Show me a record and a field on your cards.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can name a record and a field. Plenary question: "What is the difference between a record and a field?"
