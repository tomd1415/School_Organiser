# Remote working and end-of-unit quiz

## Today we are learning
- explain why mental and physical well-being matter
- describe a good and a bad remote work space (ergonomics)
- answer questions about the whole unit

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: well-being · ergonomics · mental health · physical health. Same routine as every lesson.

## Starter — well-being
![A brain and a heart balanced on a see-saw]({{res:l6-well-being-balance-mind-and-body-teach-computing.png}})

- WELL-BEING = feeling and being healthy and happy
- MENTAL well-being = how we feel and cope
- PHYSICAL well-being = our body and health

> 🧑‍🏫 Links to the starter sort. Keep the two kinds of well-being clearly separate on the board.

## Technology — good and bad for us
- GOOD: reminders, breaks, research, collaboration, problem-solving games
- BAD: less movement, addictive checking, snacking and sitting too long

> 🧑‍🏫 Matches the activity card sort. Draw on pupils' own experience gently — no pressure to share.

## A healthy work space (ergonomics)
- a supportive chair at the right height
- screen at eye level (not too low)
- good lighting and back/wrist support
- a quiet area, away from distractions

> 🧑‍🏫 ERGONOMICS = designing a space to be safe and comfortable. Likely error: a low laptop on the sofa → neck strain. Links to the "rate this work space" task.

## End-of-unit quiz

> 🧑‍🏫 Hand out the end-of-unit quiz worksheet. Reassure: it covers everything we have done. Calm, exam-style conditions. Self-mark or peer-mark after.
