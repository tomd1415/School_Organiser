# The modern world of work

## Today we are learning
- describe how the workplace has changed with technology
- compare a traditional workplace and a modern workplace
- describe advantages and disadvantages of 24/7/365 availability
- explain accessibility and inclusivity

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: stakeholder · organisation · accessibility · inclusivity · 24/7/365. Same routine as every lesson.

## Three real workers
- Carol used pen, paper, a typewriter and a filing cabinet, and commuted every day.
- Chen started in a slow office and now works from home, using email and video calls.
- Ellie is a freelance animator who works from home and finds jobs on an online platform.

> 🧑‍🏫 These are case studies (not anyone in the room). Use them to draw out changes over time. Take a few in pairs before moving on.

## The traditional workplace
- everyone travels to one office (a commute)
- formal workwear, fixed 9-to-5 hours
- slow, labour-intensive tasks: filing, posting letters, manual checks

> 🧑‍🏫 Name the cost of the commute and the slowness of the post. Common misconception: "older means worse" — it worked, it was just slower.

## The modern workplace
![Online, global working — a search bar over a world map]({{res:l1-modern-global-online-working-teach-computing.png}})

- work from anywhere; flexible hours
- instant email and video calls
- hire the best people from around the world

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the "24/7/365" idea — always reachable. Ask for one good and one bad thing (links to the Core question).

## Advantage or disadvantage?
- ADVANTAGE: faster tasks, diverse teams, easy collaboration
- DISADVANTAGE: miscommunication through screens, isolation, jobs replaced by technology

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the activity-worksheet card sort. Likely error: calling "work any time" only good — push them to see the downside (never switching off).

## Accessibility and inclusivity
- ACCESSIBILITY = changes so disabled people can work (e.g. a screen reader)
- INCLUSIVITY = treating all kinds of people fairly and hiring widely

> 🧑‍🏫 These two are easily mixed up. Accessibility = disability + reasonable adjustments. Inclusivity = a wide range of people. Keep both words on the board.
