## Starter — words matter
"Artificial" = made by humans to copy something natural. "Intelligent" is harder to pin down.

> 🧑‍🏫 Choose on the starter worksheet, then gather synonyms for "intelligent" on the board. There is no single neat definition — that is the point.

## Can a machine think?
AI = machines doing tasks that seem to need human thinking. Machine learning = the machine LEARNS from examples instead of being told every rule.

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do: which everyday things use AI? (voice assistants, photo tagging, recommendations, maps). Keep it concrete and calm.

## Programming or machine learning?
- Programming: you write the exact rules
- Machine learning: the machine learns patterns from data

> 🧑‍🏫 Card-sort on the activity worksheet. The key difference: who makes the rules — the coder, or the data.

## Thinking beyond "cool"
The machine is only as good as the examples it was trained on. That raises fairness questions.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary discussion + the confidence slider. One moral example: an AI trained on unfair examples makes unfair decisions.
