# What is IT?

## Today we are learning
- name some computers
- name the parts of a computer
- say what a computer is used for
- sort things into IT and not IT

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: computer, IT (information technology). Same calm routine as every lesson. No sound or flashing.

## IT means information technology
- IT is short for **information technology**
- IT means a **computer**, or something that **works with a computer**
- a laptop, a tablet and a desktop are all computers

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep it concrete. "IT = a computer, or something that works with a computer." Say the full words once, then just use "IT".

## What can a computer help us do?
- write
- paint a picture
- play a game
- watch a video

> 🧑‍🏫 Think, pair, share. Add their ideas. This matches the Core "tick all" question. The catch option is "grow a tree by itself" — a computer cannot do that.

## IT or not IT?
- is it a computer?
- does it work with a computer?
- if YES, it is IT

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the card-sort on the activity worksheet. A printer and a smart speaker are IT (they work with a computer). A pencil and a chair are not IT. Likely error: "a pencil is IT" — fix-words "a pencil does not work with a computer."
