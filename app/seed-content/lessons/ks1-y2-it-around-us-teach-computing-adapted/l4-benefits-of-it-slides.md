# The benefits of IT

## Today we are learning
- recognise common types of IT
- show how IT devices work together
- say why we use IT

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Recap: IT means a computer, or something that works with a computer. Same routine.

## IT in a supermarket
![A barcode]({{res:l4-barcode.jpg}})

- a **barcode** is the lines on a product
- a **scanner** reads the barcode
- the price then shows on a screen

> 🧑‍🏫 Each barcode is a number for one product. The scanner reads it very fast. Starter: the scanner finds the PRICE.

## Working together at the till
- the scanner reads the barcode
- the computer looks up the price
- the screen shows the price
- the price is added to the bill

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the "put the steps in order" task on the worksheet. The big idea: each piece of IT does one job, and TOGETHER they do the whole job.

## You be the till
- one person is the **shopper**
- one person is the **scanner**
- one person is the **price finder**

> 🧑‍🏫 The role-play (I-do / we-do / you-do). Focus on the PROCESS, not the maths. Swap roles so everyone tries each part. They photograph their receipt for show-your-work. Likely error: trying to do all jobs at once — fix-words "each part does just one job."

## Why do we use IT?
- it saves people time
- it does jobs quickly
- it helps us not forget things

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the Challenge "tick all". Think, pair, share. The catch option is "makes the weather sunny."
