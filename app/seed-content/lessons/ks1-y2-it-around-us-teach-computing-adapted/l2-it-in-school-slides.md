# IT in school

## Today we are learning
- find IT in our school
- sort school IT by what it is used for
- say that some IT can be used in more than one way

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Recap from last lesson: IT means a computer, or something that works with a computer. Same routine.

## Is it IT?
![A computer keyboard]({{res:l2-a-computer-keyboard-teach-computing.png}})

- a keyboard is IT — it works with a computer
- a printer is IT — it works with a computer
- a teddy bear is **not** IT

> 🧑‍🏫 Thumbs up / thumbs down for each picture on the starter. Keep it quick and calm. Likely error: "a teddy is IT" — fix-words "it does not work with a computer."

## IT in our school
- tablets and computers
- the interactive whiteboard
- a printer

> 🧑‍🏫 Look around the room together. List the IT you can see. This feeds the Core "name one piece of IT" question.

## What is the IT used for?
- **write or make** — keyboard, printer
- **find things out** — tablet, whiteboard
- **play and learn** — games, learning apps

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the card-sort on the activity worksheet (three jobs). Model one: "we use a printer to print our work."

## Some IT does more than one job
- a tablet can find things out
- a tablet can also play a game
- a tablet can also take a photo

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the Challenge "tick all" question. Big idea: the same device can do more than one job. The catch option is "make a sandwich."
