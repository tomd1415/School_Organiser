# Using IT safely

## Today we are learning
- list different uses of IT
- talk about rules for using IT
- say how rules keep us safe

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Calm, low-arousal. This lesson touches online safety — follow the school online-safety policy if anything is disclosed.

## What can we do with IT?
![A smart TV with lots of apps]({{res:l5-a-smart-tv-with-apps-teach-computing.png}})

- watch videos
- play games
- send a message
- paint a picture

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter: tick all the things you can do with a tablet. List the devices pupils use at home (TV, tablet, console). Keep it short.

## There are rules for using IT
- ask before taking someone's photo
- take a break from the screen
- tell an adult if something worries you

> 🧑‍🏫 These are the SAFE choices in the card-sort. Read each rule aloud so everyone understands it.

## Some choices are not safe
- telling people your password
- looking at things that are too old for you
- carrying a tablet while running

> 🧑‍🏫 These are the NOT-SAFE choices in the card-sort. Keep it matter-of-fact, not scary. Likely error: thinking "share your password" is kind/helpful — fix-words "a password is a secret."

## How do rules keep us safe?
- asking first keeps people happy
- a break rests our eyes and body
- telling an adult means we get help

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the Core matching task (rule → why it helps). Model one: "Taking a break keeps me safe because it rests my eyes."

## Be safe and kind with photos
- would the person be happy?
- did you ask first?
- is anyone in the background?

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Big idea: be safe AND respectful. This sets up Lesson 6 (using IT in different ways).
