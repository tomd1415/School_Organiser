# IT in the world

## Today we are learning
- find examples of IT in the world
- sort IT by where it is found
- talk about what IT is used for

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Recap: IT means a computer, or something that works with a computer. Same routine.

## IT is all around us
- IT is not only in school
- we find IT in shops
- we find IT on the street
- lots of jobs use IT

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter: thumbs up / down for "where might we find IT?" (shop, woods, street, sea). IT could be almost anywhere a person takes a phone, but it is MORE likely in some places.

## IT in a shop
![A card machine in a shop]({{res:l3-card-reader.jpg}})

- a till adds up what you buy
- a card machine takes the payment
- a barcode scanner reads the price

## IT on the street
![A cash machine]({{res:l3-cash-machine.jpg}})

- a cash machine gives you money
- a crossing button helps you cross safely
- a speed camera checks cars are not too fast

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the other card-sort group. The crossing button matches the Challenge "tick all" question.

## Talk about IT
![A crossing button]({{res:l3-crossing-button.jpg}})

- where is it found?
- what does it do?
- how does it help us?

> 🧑‍🏫 Model the Core sentence: "A crossing button is on the street. It helps us cross safely." Scaffolding: let pupils point at the picture as they talk.

## IT is nearly everywhere
- most jobs use IT
- even a phone is IT
- IT helps people do their jobs

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary idea: can you think of a job with NO IT at all? Most jobs use some IT, even just a phone.
