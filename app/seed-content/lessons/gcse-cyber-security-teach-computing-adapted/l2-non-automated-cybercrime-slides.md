- explain what social engineering is
- spot the warning signs of a phishing email

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: social engineering · phishing · pharming · shoulder surfing · tailgating · blagging.

## Starter — looking over a shoulder
![Looking over a shoulder to read a password]({{res:l2-shoulder-surfing.png}})

What is the person at the back doing?

> 🧑‍🏫 Draw out: they are reading the password. This is "shoulder surfing." People — not computers — are the weak link today.

## What is social engineering?  (I do)
Social engineering is tricking PEOPLE into giving away secret information or doing something unsafe.

> 🧑‍🏫 Key idea to stress: it targets humans, not machines. Give one calm example — a fake "IT support" phone call.

## Spot the phishing email  (we do)
Warning signs: spelling mistakes · a strange link · urgent threats · asking for your password.

> 🧑‍🏫 The real email has many clues. Work through two or three together, then pairs tick the warning signs on the activity sheet.

## How a phishing attack works  (you do)
Fake email → click the link → fake website → you type your password → the criminal has it.

> 🧑‍🏫 Pairs drag the four steps into order on the sheet. Likely error: mixing up the order — fix-words "the email always comes first."

## More tricks: in person or far away?
Some need the criminal there (shoulder surfing, tailgating). Some work from far away (phishing, pharming).

> 🧑‍🏫 Pairs sort the tricks on the activity sheet. Invite calm real-life examples they may have seen.
