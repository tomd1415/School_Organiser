## What is a penetration tester?  (I do)
A penetration tester is an ethical hacker, paid to find a company's weak spots BEFORE criminals do.

> 🧑‍🏫 Key idea: they have permission. The point is to fix the weak spots, then write a report.

## The steps of a penetration test  (we do)
Plan and gather information → scan for weak spots → try to break in → write a report.

> 🧑‍🏫 Pairs order the four steps on the activity sheet. Stress the final report — finding the weak spot is only useful if it gets fixed.

## Three ways to attack — and test  (you do)
- Physical — getting into the building.
- Software — passwords and unpatched systems.
- Social engineering — tricking the people.

> 🧑‍🏫 Pairs plan tests for the made-up company 'Ranter' and sort them on the sheet. Encourage their own ideas from earlier lessons.

## Other ways to find weak spots
Network forensics studies an attack afterwards. Commercial analysis tools scan a network automatically.

> 🧑‍🏫 Mention free tools exist (e.g. NCSC Web Check) but a human tester sees more.
