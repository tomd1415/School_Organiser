# Comparing people

## Today we are learning
- describe people using attributes
- choose an attribute to compare people
- collect data and make a pictogram
- say what my pictogram shows

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: attribute · compare · collect · pictogram · most common · conclusion. Pictogram software open. Use cohort-level data only — never record a single pupil's personal details.

## Last lesson
- an attribute describes an object
- we grouped objects by an attribute
- then tallied and made a pictogram

> 🧑‍🏫 Quick recap. Ask one pupil for an attribute of an object.

## People have attributes

![A face with red hair]({{res:l5-a-face-with-red-hair-teach-computing.png}})

- hair colour, wears glasses, number of pets
- some attributes you can see, some you cannot
- we can sort people by one attribute

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: thinking everyone fits one group → fix-words "every person goes in exactly one group." The worksheet sorts four faces by "wears glasses".

## Collect the data
- choose a question: "What is the most common hair colour?"
- pick the answers people can choose from
- collect the data, then make a pictogram

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Keep it cohort-level — count how many, do not write down who. Build the pictogram on the computer.

## Draw a conclusion
- read the most common and the least common
- "Most people in our class have brown hair."
- that sentence is a **conclusion**

> 🧑‍🏫 Model a conclusion sentence. Pupils write a conclusion about their own pictogram and screenshot it.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Share one conclusion.
