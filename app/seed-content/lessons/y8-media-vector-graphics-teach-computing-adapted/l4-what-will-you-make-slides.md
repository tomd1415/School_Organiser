# What will you make?

## Today we are learning
- describe logos, icons, and illustrations
- choose a project and plan a design
- combine tools and techniques to create a vector image
- evaluate a project against its purpose

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: logo · icon · illustration · plan · purpose · rubric.

## Starter — exit tickets
Look back at last lesson's key words. Agree, disagree, or add to each one.

> 🧑‍🏫 Read out a few exit tickets. It does not matter if they were right — discuss and improve the descriptions together.

- logo = a symbol for a company or product
- icon = a small, simple picture on a button
- illustration = a drawing for a book, poster, or flyer

> 🧑‍🏫 Sort the examples together on the worksheet. Vectors suit all three because they stay sharp at any size.

## Choose your project
- Project 1 — icons for a zoo website
- Project 2 — a logo for a space theme park
- Project 3 — illustrations for a children's book

> 🧑‍🏫 Pairs choose one. You may set partners or let them pick. Encourage questions.

## Plan your design
- sketch your idea first
- check the rubric — what is the purpose?

> 🧑‍🏫 Talk through the rubric if it is new to them. Draw their attention to the "design" row. They can research for inspiration.

## Create your vector
- use the skills from Lessons 1–3
- apply a few skills well, not many poorly

> 🧑‍🏫 30 minutes of paired making. One computer, take turns guiding. They screenshot their progress.

## How are you getting on?
- one thing that was easy
- one thing that was tricky
- give each other one tip

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary with another pair. They also rate their confidence on the worksheet.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me your project so far.
