# What is pre-production?

## Today we are learning
- describe what pre-production means
- name pre-production planning tools
- choose a planning tool for a media product
- create a piece of planning

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: pre-production · mood board · mind map · storyboard · script · visualisation. Same routine as every lesson.

## Starter — a new job
- a client has built a new home robot
- they want a website to sell it
- what should we ask them BEFORE we start?

> 🧑‍🏫 Take a few ideas: budget, target market, number of pages, logo, deadline. Land on the word "pre-production" = the planning stage. This matches the starter worksheet choice.

## The three stages
- PRE-production — plan it
- PRODUCTION — make it
- POST-production — finish it and release it

> 🧑‍🏫 Use a video as the example: plan it (storyboard) → film it → edit it. The starter worksheet asks pupils to drag these three into order.

## Pre-production tool — mood board
![A mood board]({{res:l1-mood-board-example-teach-computing.jpg}})

- pictures and colours that show the "feel"
- helps everyone agree on the look before we start

> 🧑‍🏫 A mood board is not the final product — it sets the mood/style. Ask: what feeling does this board give you?

## Pre-production tool — storyboard
![A hand-drawn storyboard]({{res:l1-storyboard-example-teach-computing.jpg}})

- boxes showing each scene of a video, in order
- shows the camera shot and what happens

> 🧑‍🏫 Point at the boxes in order. Each box = one shot. This is the tool they will use for the robot video.

## Pre-production tool — script and visualisation
![A film script]({{res:l1-film-script-close-up-teach-computing.jpg}})

- a SCRIPT is the words that will be said
- a VISUALISATION is a quick drawing of an idea (like the car sketch on the activity sheet)

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep it concrete: script = words; visualisation = a rough picture of an idea (logo, layout, product).

## Your turn — make a mind map
- in your group, mind map the robot website
- think: budget · who it is for · pages · logo · deadline

> 🧑‍🏫 Groups of 3–5, one big sheet. TA: prompt with the headings, do not write it for them. No right answer — reward ideas.

## Your turn — make a storyboard
- plan a short video about the robot
- one box per scene, with a title and the camera shot

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the storyboard template. Likely error: writing a paragraph instead of drawing boxes — fix-words: "one box = one shot." Photo the work onto the activity worksheet.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me one planning tool you made.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can name and use at least two pre-production tools.
