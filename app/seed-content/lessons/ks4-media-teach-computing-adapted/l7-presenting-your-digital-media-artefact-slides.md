# Presenting your digital media artefact

## Today we are learning
- present your work and explain your design choices
- evaluate what worked and what to improve
- assess another group fairly using the rubric
- show what you learned across the unit

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: peer assessment · rubric · weighting · reflection · evaluate. Final lesson — keep it calm and supportive.

## Starter — peer assessment
![A person presenting results]({{res:l7-presenting-results-teach-computing.png}})

- you will grade other groups against the rubric
- listen, then circle the level that fits
- judge the WORK, be fair and kind

> 🧑‍🏫 Hand out a rubric per pupil per group. Write the presenting group's names at the top, not your own. The starter worksheet checks they grade the work, not the people.

## Present your artefact
- show what you made
- explain the PROCESS you went through
- explain your design CHOICES and any problems you solved

> 🧑‍🏫 If a group misses a rubric area, ask a prompting question so they can show what they forgot. Those not presenting fill in the rubric.

## Evaluate — what would you do differently?
- what worked well?
- what would you change next time?

> 🧑‍🏫 This reflection is on the end-of-unit quiz worksheet, alongside quiz questions covering the whole unit (pre-production, graphics, video, websites).

## The unit journey
- pre-production: plan it (mood board, storyboard, script)
- create it: graphics, video, website
- plan, produce and present a real artefact

> 🧑‍🏫 Recap the whole journey so they see how the lessons connected into the final project.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Tell me one thing you are proud of and one thing you'd change.

> 🧑‍🏫 Collect the rubrics and use the calculator for final grades. Note who can evaluate their own design choices.
