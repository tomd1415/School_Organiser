# Creating digital video

## Today we are learning
- name camera angles used in video
- describe video properties: FPS, compression, file format
- use OpenShot to edit a short video
- export a finished video file

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: camera angle · frames per second · compression · codec · container · transition · export. Headphones out and ready.

## Starter — camera angles
- CLOSE-UP — subject fills the screen
- LONG SHOT — the whole scene, far away (a "wide shot")
- HIGH ANGLE — looking down, makes the subject look small/weak
- LOW ANGLE — looking up, makes the subject look big/powerful

> 🧑‍🏫 Demo each with your hand as the "camera". The starter worksheet matches angles to descriptions and labels the parts of a camera.

## How video works — frames
- video = lots of still pictures shown fast, like a flip book
- frames per second (FPS) = how many pictures each second
- TV is about 24 FPS; HD is 30+ FPS

> 🧑‍🏫 Teacher-played hook: a short flip-book clip (motion only, ~30s). It has motion — play it only if it suits the class. Then connect to FPS.

## Compression and file formats
- COMPRESSION makes the file smaller, but loses a little quality
- the file format (MP4, AVI, MOV) is the CONTAINER
- the CODEC is the instructions that squash/unsquash the data

![A file going into a container box]({{res:l3-file-container-teach-computing.png}})

> 🧑‍🏫 MP4 is the everyday standard: small file, good quality. This is the fill-in-the-blank on the activity worksheet.

## Edit in OpenShot

- drag clips onto tracks

## Export your video
- when it is finished, EXPORT it as an MP4
- exporting turns your edit into one playable video file

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: closing without exporting (the .osp is the project, not the video) — fix-words: "save the project AND export the video."
