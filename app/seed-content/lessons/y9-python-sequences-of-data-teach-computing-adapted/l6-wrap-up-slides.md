# Wrap up — finish, show and reflect

## Today we are learning
- finish and show my mini-project
- read list and string code and predict its output
- complete a loop that goes through a sequence
- reflect on what I have learned this unit

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Calm wrap-up lesson: finish, show, reflect. Same routine as every lesson.

## Finish your mini-project
Carry on from last lesson, or explore a second project if yours is done.

> 🧑‍🏫 Give quiet working time. Fast finishers can showcase their program or try a second project. Praise working programs, however small.

## Show what you know
Read short programs about days, planets and the alphabet and say what they do.

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the activity worksheet as the assessment. Allow scrap paper to work answers out. Support: matching. Core: fill the gap. Challenge: read and fix the planets condition.

## Order the program  (everyone)
A loop that prints each letter's position in the alphabet.

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the Parsons task. It pulls together input, a `for` loop over a string, and `index` — the whole unit in one program.

## A peek ahead — images are sequences too
A digital image is a long sequence of pixels. A `for` loop over the pixels can change every colour.

> 🧑‍🏫 Optional showcase for fast finishers: a colour-filter program loops over pixels just like a list. No new task required — it shows that "sequences of data" reach much further than this unit.

## Reflection
Tell me one thing you learned, and rate your confidence with lists and strings.

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the reflection box + confidence slider on the worksheet. Sticky notes work well too: one thing learned, one surprise, one thing to improve.
