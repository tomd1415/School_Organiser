# Directions

## Today we are learning
- follow an instruction
- think of words we can act out
- give clear directions to a partner

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: instructions · directions. Same routine as every lesson.

## Instructions
- last lesson we pressed buttons to give the robot instructions
- the buttons are how we tell the robot what to do
- today WE will give instructions to each other

> 🧑‍🏫 Slide 3. Link back to Lesson 1: the buttons gave the robot instructions. Today the instructions are words, not buttons.

## You are the robot
- I give one instruction at a time
- you do ONLY that instruction
- example: stand up · sit down · nod · clap once

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1. No moving-around instructions yet. Keep wording tiny and exact. You may repeat the same instruction. Likely error: pupils add extra actions — fix-words: "only do what I said."

## Words we can act out
- think of words that are instructions
- say the word and show what it looks like
- we will make a class list

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 2. Build the class list; add a small picture beside each word so non-readers can use it. Keep moving words in a separate list for the next slide.

## Giving directions
- a clear instruction is small and exact
- "step" is better than "walk"
- "walk" never says when to stop

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 3. This is the subject-knowledge point: precise instructions like a robot. If pupils say left/right but mix them up, allow "turn" and point the way. Space to move safely.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Give me one clear instruction.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who gives a single, clear, small instruction and who follows one exactly.
