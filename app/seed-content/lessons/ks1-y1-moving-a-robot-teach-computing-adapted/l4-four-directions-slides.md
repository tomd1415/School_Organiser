# Four directions

## Today we are learning
- compare left turns and right turns
- use turn and move commands together
- predict where a program of up to four commands ends

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: left · right · turn · commands. Same routine as every lesson.

## Move it
![The buttons on top of the floor robot]({{res:l4-floor-robot-buttons-teach-computing.png}})

- last lesson: Forwards and Backwards
- today we add Left turn and Right turn
- find the two turn buttons

> 🧑‍🏫 Slide 3. Recap forwards = one square forward, backwards = one square back. Then point out the two turn arrows.

## In a spin
- try each turn button (Clear in between)
- the robot turns ON THE SPOT
- it does not move to a new square

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1. The key misconception: some pupils think a turn also moves the robot forward one square. It does not — it turns on the spot. Fix-words: "a turn changes which way it faces, not which square."

## Left and right
- a Left turn and a Right turn turn the SAME amount
- one faces left, one faces right
- watch the robot carefully

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1 finish. If pupils mix up left/right, that is fine — focus on "same amount, opposite ways." Use the robot's eyes to show the new facing direction.

## Work it out
- put the robot on the start square, facing the top
- read the program one command at a time
- point to each square as you go
- mark where you think it stops, then run it

> 🧑‍🏫 Activities 2–3. Trial and error for the maps; then the prediction worksheet. Encourage pointing/paper-bot to step through. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Show me a turn and a move.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary: the key difference is turns happen on the spot. Note who can step through a four-command program.
