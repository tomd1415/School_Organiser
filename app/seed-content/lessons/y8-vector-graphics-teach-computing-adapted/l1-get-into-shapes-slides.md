# Get into shapes

## Today we are learning
- name vector graphics and the software we use
- draw and change shapes with the shape tools
- change a shape's fill and outline
- move, rotate, and order shapes (z-order)

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Same routine as every lesson. Vocabulary on the board: vector · fill · stroke · rotate · z-order · handle.

## Starter — a picture made of shapes
![A house built from simple shapes]({{res:l1-shapes-house-teach-computing.png}})

Think · pair · share. On your starter, tick all the shapes you can see.

> 🧑‍🏫 Draw out that a whole picture is made of separate simple shapes. The photograph option is the wrong one — a vector is shapes, not a photo.

## What is a vector graphic?
- a drawing made of lines and shapes
- used for logos, icons, and illustrations
- the house above is an example

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep it concrete: logos (badges), icons (little buttons), illustrations (cartoons). All built from shapes.

## The software — Inkscape
![The Inkscape logo]({{res:l1-inkscape-logo-teach-computing.png}})

- open source = free to use and share
- cross-platform = works on Windows, Mac, and Linux

> 🧑‍🏫 Explain open source and cross-platform plainly. Everyone should have Inkscape open with a saved .svg before Activity 1.

## Draw shapes (I do → you do)
![A square, an ellipse, and a polygon]({{res:l1-three-shapes-teach-computing.png}})

Three tools: rectangle, ellipse, and star/polygon. Hold Ctrl to make a perfect square or circle.

> 🧑‍🏫 Model each tool once, then pairs draw at least four shapes. Likely error: tool stays selected — fix-words "switch to the black arrow to select". TA: prompt, do not draw it for them.

## Change fill and outline
![Shapes with different fills and outlines]({{res:l1-fills-outlines-teach-computing.png}})

![The Fill and Stroke tabs]({{res:l1-fill-stroke-tabs-teach-computing.png}})

- fill = the colour inside
- stroke = the line around the edge

> 🧑‍🏫 Show Fill, Stroke paint, Stroke style. New shapes keep the last colours used. Challenge: change the dash style of the outline.

## Move, rotate, and order (z-order)
- drag to move · click twice then drag a corner to rotate
- z-order = which shape is in front or behind

> 🧑‍🏫 Each new shape sits on top of the older ones. Use Raise/Lower to change z-order. They paste a screenshot of their shapes on the activity worksheet.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me your saved shapes.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who changed fill, outline, and z-order. Movement break is routine, not a reward.
