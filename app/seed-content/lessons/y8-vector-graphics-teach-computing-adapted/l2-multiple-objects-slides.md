# Working with multiple objects

## Today we are learning
- select more than one object at once
- align and distribute objects
- group and ungroup objects
- combine shapes with union, difference, and intersection

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: object · align · distribute · group · combine · union · difference · intersection.

## Starter — stacks of shapes
A flag with three coloured stripes is just rectangles stacked up.

> 🧑‍🏫 Draw out: a picture is made of many objects, stacked in a z-order. On the starter, a 3-stripe flag = 3 rectangles. A harder flag could need more.

## Why work with many objects?
- saves time
- keeps an image neat and even
- lets you move parts together

> 🧑‍🏫 Last lesson was one shape at a time. Today: several at once. Remind them "object" = any item added.

## Align and distribute
- align = line objects up (left, centre, top)
- distribute = space objects out evenly

> 🧑‍🏫 Model select-many first (rubber-band drag, or Shift-click). Likely error: only one object selected — fix-words "drag a box around them all first".

## Group and ungroup
![Two objects grouped and selected together]({{res:l2-grouped-objects-teach-computing.png}})

- group = treat several objects as one
- ungroup = split them back apart

> 🧑‍🏫 Group then move/copy together. Stress: a group can be ungrouped any time — combining cannot.

## Combine — union, difference, intersection
![A crescent made by difference]({{res:l2-difference-crescent-teach-computing.png}})

- union = keep all of both shapes
- difference = remove the top shape from the one below
- intersection = keep only the overlap

> 🧑‍🏫 This crescent = two circles, difference. Combining makes ONE new object that cannot be ungrouped (only Undo straight after). They screenshot their result.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me your aligned, grouped, or combined objects.

> 🧑‍🏫 Quick hands-up plenary: who can name what union/difference/intersection do?
