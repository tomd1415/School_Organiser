## Vectors vs bitmaps
![A vector clownfish]({{res:l6-vector-clownfish-teach-computing.png}})

![A photo of a clownfish — a bitmap]({{res:l6-photo-clownfish-teach-computing.jpg}})

- vector = paths and shapes, stays sharp at any size
- bitmap = a real photo, lots of fine detail

> 🧑‍🏫 Draw out: the photo holds detail a vector cannot; the vector scales without going blurry.

## A bitmap is made of pixels
![A photo zoomed in to show pixels]({{res:l6-bitmap-pixels-butterfly-teach-computing.png}})

- pixel = a tiny square of colour
- zoom in and the squares show

> 🧑‍🏫 This is why a photo blurs when enlarged — the pixels get bigger. Ask: are fonts vector or bitmap? (Vector — they scale.)

## Which type suits which use?
- vectors: logos, icons, road signs, simple cartoons
- bitmaps: photographs, detailed pictures, selfies
- sometimes either works

> 🧑‍🏫 Pairs build two lists on the worksheet (card sort). Some uses fit both.
