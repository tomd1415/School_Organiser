# Under attack — defend the network

## Today we are learning
- recall the main ways to protect against cyberattacks
- match an incident to the type of attack
- choose the protection that would stop each attack
- pick the protections that stop the most attacks

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. This lesson reviews the unit before the end-of-unit assessment. Keep every scenario about "a company / a worker", never a real pupil.

## Starter — recall the protections
![A firewall protection card]({{res:l6-protection-firewall-card.jpg}})

In pairs, list every protection you can remember. Which stops the most attacks?

> 🧑‍🏫 Build a class list. Don't settle the "most attacks" question yet — they'll see it during the game.

## The Under Attack game  (you do)
![A ransomware attack card]({{res:l6-attack-ransomware-card.jpg}})

You are the network manager of an online shop, on a tight budget. For each incident: name the attack, choose the protection.

> 🧑‍🏫 Play in small groups using the activity worksheet table. Support: match from a list. Challenge: justify the cheapest protection that still works.

## Attack or protection?
![A two-factor authentication card]({{res:l6-protection-2fa-card.jpg}})

Sort each card into Attack or Protection.

> 🧑‍🏫 The card sort on the worksheet. Reflect: two-factor authentication and anti-malware stopped the most attacks — and you can turn 2FA on at home for free.
