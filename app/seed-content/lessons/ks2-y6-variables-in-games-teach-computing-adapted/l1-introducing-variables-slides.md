# Introducing variables

## Today we are learning
- a variable is something that can change
- things that change in a game, like a score
- a variable can hold a number or letters

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud, slowly. Words on the board: variable · change · name · value. Same routine as every lesson.

## Starter — the football score
- in a match, the **score** changes when a goal is scored
- the **time** changes as the game goes on
- the pitch size stays the same

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils tick what changes on the starter worksheet. Likely error: "the pitch changes." Fix-words: "a variable is something that CHANGES." Support pupils choose what a variable is.

## A variable can change
- a thing that can change is a **variable**
- the score is a variable: it goes up and down
- a variable holds one value at a time (one score at a time)

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep it concrete: a team has ONE score at any moment. When they score, the new score replaces the old one.

## Changing the score in Scratch
![A change score by 2 block]({{res:l1-change-score-by-2.png}})

- the **change score by** block changes the score
- a positive number makes it go up (by 2)
- a negative number (-2) makes it go down

> 🧑‍🏫 Demonstrate clicking the balls in the Score change project. Support choose what each block does. Challenge: what does -2 do?

## Make your own variable
![The Scratch editor]({{res:l1-scratch-editor.png}})

- click **Variables** → **Make a Variable**
- type a clear name, like Score
- pupils order these steps on the activity worksheet

> 🧑‍🏫 Model it on the board once, then pupils do it. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Numbers or letters
- a variable can hold a **number** (a score: 5)
- or **letters** (a team name: Japan)
- both are called the **value**

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils sort values into number/letters on the activity worksheet. Then they change the score and screenshot it.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Tell me one thing: what is a variable?

> 🧑‍🏫 Listen for "something that can change". Movement break before the next lesson.
