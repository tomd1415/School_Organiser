# Poster making

## Today we are learning
- plan a poster that conveys a message
- choose and download a suitable image
- create a poster in a publishing app

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on board: portrait · landscape · download · heading · body text.

## Starter — good or not so good?
![A "Mobile Threats" online safety poster]({{res:l2-mobile-threats-poster-teach-computing.png}})

What is good about this poster? What could be better?

> 🧑‍🏫 Remind them of the class "good poster features" list from Lesson 1. Pupils answer the starter worksheet. Design is personal — answers vary if they can explain.

## Plan first, then make  (I do)
A plan helps you make good choices BEFORE you open the app.

> 🧑‍🏫 Model filling in main message → main colour → portrait/landscape → image theme. Stress: everything should help the message.

## Choosing colours  (we do)
A colour tool gives you matching companion colours. Black or white text is often best.

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the free colour tool. Pupils note colour codes on their plan worksheet. Support: "use black or white text" as a safe default.

## Find and download an image  (you do)
Search for an image that matches your theme. Check you are allowed to use it. Download it.

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: takes the image from the poster they chose → fix-words "find your OWN licensed image". Use policy-checked image sites.
