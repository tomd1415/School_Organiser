# Adding content

## Today we are learning
- search for suitable text for slides
- search for and add a suitable image
- check content against the rubric

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on board: content · licence · source. Keep a BLANK copy of the styled slides too.

## Starter — what would you change?
Your styled slides are still mostly empty. What content do they need?

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils tick on the starter worksheet: charity name, key message, facts, a matching image.

## The task — three slides of content  (I do)
One slide: the charity name. One: a key message. One: two or three facts with an image.

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the content parts of the rubric (grey rows). The charity asked for NO animations.

## Find and write the text  (we do)
Search for facts. Write them in YOUR OWN words. Add a source for any statistic or quote.

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: copies text word-for-word → fix-words "in your own words, then add the source". Support: sentence starters.

## Find and add an image  (you do)
Find a licensed image that MATCHES your text. Add it and resize it, keeping its proportions.

> 🧑‍🏫 "Appropriate" = age + theme + permission. Resize from a corner so it doesn't stretch. Pupils screenshot on the worksheet.

## Give feedback with the rubric  (you do)
Review a partner's slides. Start positive. Be specific. Suggest one improvement.

> 🧑‍🏫 Model rubric-based feedback. Pupils may reply to a comment if they disagree, with a reason.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Show me your slides with content.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who added their own-words facts with a source and a matching image.
