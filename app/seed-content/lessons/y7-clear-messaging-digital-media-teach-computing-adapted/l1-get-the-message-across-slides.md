# Get the message across

## Today we are learning
- choose search terms for a topic
- screenshot an image into another program
- spot what makes a good poster

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Same routine as every lesson. Vocabulary on board: search term · screenshot · annotate.

## Starter — what does this sign tell you?
![A blue "one way" road sign with an arrow]({{res:l1-one-way-road-sign-teach-computing.jpg}})

Talk to your partner. What is the message? How do you know?

> 🧑‍🏫 Think · pair · share. Draw out: a picture (arrow) + a few words = a fast, clear message. Pupils answer the starter worksheet.

## What makes a good poster?  (I do)
A good poster has a clear message, a matching picture, and easy-to-read text.

> 🧑‍🏫 Build a class list of "good poster features". Keep it on the board — Lesson 2 uses it. Tick-all question on the starter worksheet.

## Search for an online safety poster  (we do)
Choose search terms. Search. Pick one poster you think is good.

> 🧑‍🏫 Use pre-checked sites if school policy needs it. Likely error: vague terms like "stuff" → fix-words "say WHAT and WHERE — online safety poster". Support: word-bank of terms.

## Your turn — screenshot and label  (you do)
Take a screenshot of your poster. Paste it into slides or a document. Add labels.

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: printed "press these keys" screenshot card. Challenge: label a feature AND say why it helps. Pupils paste the screenshot on the activity worksheet.

Tick your three "I can…". Show me your labelled screenshot.
