# Presenting

## Today we are learning
- plan how to deliver a presentation
- explain your work to others
- evaluate your work against the rubric

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on board: present · design choices.

## Starter — a good presentation
What makes a good presentation? What makes a good presenter?

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils tick on the starter worksheet. Draw out: simple, planned, practised; speak clearly, look up, smile.

## Plan your talk  (I do → we do)
Plan a two-minute talk. Say one thing about each slide. Explain your design choices.

> 🧑‍🏫 Model one slide aloud. Pupils fill the plan worksheet, then practise with a partner. Support: one sentence per slide.

## Present your slides  (you do)
Present to a small group. Imagine you are presenting to the charity.

> 🧑‍🏫 Group flexibly for your room. Offer a calm option (present to one peer / to the TA) for anyone who finds the class too much. Supervise.

## Evaluate against the rubric
Mark the rubric statements you have met for branding AND content.

> 🧑‍🏫 Self-assessment. Support them gently — they may under-rate. Look at blank slides (branding) then content slides.

## Working between applications
You used a web browser, a publishing app, a graphics editor, and presentation software.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary: name the apps used and how you moved text/images (copy-and-paste, save-and-load). Tie back to working safely online.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Tell me one design choice you are proud of.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who presented and who self-evaluated honestly against the rubric.
