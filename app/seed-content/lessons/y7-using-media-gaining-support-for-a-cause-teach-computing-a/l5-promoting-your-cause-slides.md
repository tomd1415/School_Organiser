# Promoting your cause

## Today we are learning
- set success criteria for a blog
- build a blog using the software
- add credible, referenced content
- lay out the blog to suit the audience

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: success criteria · audience · blog · feedback. Decide your blog software in advance (Google Sites / Microsoft Sway); cheat sheets are provided.

## Starter — success criteria  (I do)
A good criterion is clear, not just an opinion.

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the starter worksheet. "Useful" is an opinion — improve it to "all sources are referenced." Likely error: vague criteria → fix-words: "could two people agree if it was met?"

## The assessment criteria  (we do)
Your blog is marked on audience, sources, formatting, and images.

![The assessment rubric: audience, sources, formatting, images]({{res:l5-assessment-rubric-teach-computing.png}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the rubric. Give each pupil a copy to keep open. These four rows are exactly what the blog needs.

## Build your blog  (you do)
Open the software, set up a page, and add your researched content.

> 🧑‍🏫 Demonstrate the software with the cheat sheet. Support: follow the cheat-sheet steps. Challenge: add a second page or extra feature. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Helpful feedback  (we do)
Good feedback is positive and says exactly what to improve.

> 🧑‍🏫 Comment A is best — positive, specific, suggests a change. Remind pupils: feedback is supportive, not critical. They record feedback on the activity worksheet.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me your blog so far.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who has referenced content and who responded to feedback. They finish next lesson.
