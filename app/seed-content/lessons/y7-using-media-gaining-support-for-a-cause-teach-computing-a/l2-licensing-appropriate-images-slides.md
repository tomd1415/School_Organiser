# Licensing appropriate images

## Today we are learning
- choose images that suit the document
- explain the copyright rule
- choose the right Creative Commons licence
- credit (reference) the source

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: copyright · licensing · Creative Commons · text wrapping · cropping · credit.

## Starter — is this a good image?
Does the image suit the purpose and the audience?

> 🧑‍🏫 Use the starter worksheet. Discuss each scenario; a single best answer each time. Likely error: "it looks nice" → fix-words: "does it match what the document is about?"

## Copyright — the rule  (I do)
You cannot use someone's image without permission. Copyright is automatic.

> 🧑‍🏫 The 3-minute copyright clip is a teacher-played hook (motion + sound — teacher's choice). Pause it and restate: using an image without permission can break the law.

## Find images you are allowed to use  (we do)
Filter your search to Creative Commons.

![A Google image search filtered to Creative Commons licences]({{res:l2-creative-commons-image-search-teach-computing.png}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Demonstrate: Tools → Usage Rights → Creative Commons. Note: test your search terms before the lesson; rely on the school filter.

## Which Creative Commons licence?  (we do)
The website builds a licence from your choices.

![The Creative Commons licence chooser showing the licence badges]({{res:l2-creative-commons-licence-chooser-teach-computing.png}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Walk through one scenario together. NonCommercial = no money. Stress that NC means "not for profit".

## Your turn — insert and credit an image  (you do)
Add an image to your document, format it, and write the credit.

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: a printed step strip for insert → crop → text wrap. Challenge: recolour and add a second image. They paste a link + screenshot on the activity worksheet.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me a credited image in your document.

> 🧑‍🏫 Peer feedback: one supportive comment + one improvement. Note who credited their image.
