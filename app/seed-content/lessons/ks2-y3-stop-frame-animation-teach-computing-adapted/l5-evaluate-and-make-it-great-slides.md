# Evaluate and make it great

## Today we are learning
- watch my animation back and say what is good
- choose one thing to make better
- delete a frame I do not want

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: evaluate · improve · delete · frame · feedback. Recap: last lesson we filmed our stories carefully.

## What makes a good animation?
- smooth movement
- the tablet stayed still
- no hands in the shot
- the same character all the way through

> 🧑‍🏫 Build this checklist together — it matches the starter worksheet. "Evaluate" means look at it and say what is good and what to improve.

## Watch it back
![The animation app watching a film back]({{res:l5-watching-an-animation-back-teach-computing.png}})

- press stop, then stop again
- watch your animation
- spot anything to fix

> 🧑‍🏫 Model watching an animation back. Likely error: adding new photos in the wrong place → fix-words "go to the end before you film more."

## Tidy it up
- you can delete a frame you do not want
- for example, a hand in the shot
- then watch it again

> 🧑‍🏫 Demonstrate deleting one frame. Some things can't be changed (sticky tack on show) — that's fine. Be kind when looking at each other's work.

## Your turn — make it great
- watch it back
- rate it 1 to 5
- pick one thing to improve
- make a better version

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: pupils evaluate and improve. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Support: rate it on the scale. Challenge: say how they will improve it.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Show me your best animation.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can name one improvement and carry it out.
