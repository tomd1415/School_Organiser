# Frame by frame

## Today we are learning
- each photo is called a frame
- find the buttons in the animation app
- take frames to make a stop-frame animation

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: stop-frame animation · frame · photo · capture · onion skinning. Recap: last lesson our flip books made pictures move.

## From paper to screen
![The animation app home screen with a 'new movie' button]({{res:l2-the-animation-app-home-screen-teach-computing.png}})

- last time we drew every page
- now we take photos instead
- each photo is a FRAME

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the home screen. Tap "new movie". This matches the order task on the starter worksheet. Keep the tablet on its stand and still.

## The capture screen
![The animation app capture screen showing a stick figure and the buttons]({{res:l2-the-animation-app-capture-screen-teach-computing.png}})

- capture takes a photo
- onion skin shows the last frame
- stop finishes filming

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do → we-do. Take a photo, move the drawing a little, take another. Point out the faint "onion skin" arm. This is the label task on the activity worksheet.

## Small changes, one photo at a time
- move the drawing only a little
- take a photo
- move a little, take another

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: moving the tablet between photos → fix-words "keep the tablet still." Support: find the capture button. Challenge: explain how onion skinning helps.

## Your turn — make it move
- take 6 to 10 frames
- a small change each time
- play it back

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: pupils film a small movement. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. The "making your animation" source clip is teacher-played only — keep it low volume.
