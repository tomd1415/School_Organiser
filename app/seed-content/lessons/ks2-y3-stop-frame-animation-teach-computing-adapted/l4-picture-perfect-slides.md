# Picture perfect

## Today we are learning
- move the character only a little between frames
- use onion skinning to line up the next frame
- spot when something is not consistent

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: consistency · onion skinning · frame · careful. Recap: last lesson we planned a simple story. Hand back the storyboards.

## Consistent means the same
- keep the same character
- keep the same background
- keep the tablet in the same place

> 🧑‍🏫 Teach the word "consistent" = keeping things the same. This is the match and multi-select on the starter worksheet.

## Small, careful changes

- move the character a tiny bit
- the onion skin shows the last frame
- then take the next photo

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the two frames. Ask what moved. This is the spot-the-change question on the activity worksheet. Likely error: big jumps → fix-words "only a little, then a photo."

## Stay consistent
- do not move other people's tablets
- do not move the background
- keep the same character all the way through

> 🧑‍🏫 The source "spot the change" clips are teacher-played only. Keep them low volume. Use them to show a jumpy film vs a smooth one.

## Your turn — film with care
- look at the onion skin
- move a little
- take a photo
- repeat

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: pupils film their story carefully. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Support: spot what moved. Challenge: say why small changes look smoother.
