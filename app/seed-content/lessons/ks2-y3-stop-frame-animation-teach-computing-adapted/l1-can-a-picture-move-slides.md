# Can a picture move?

## Today we are learning
- an animation is a sequence of pictures
- put pictures in order to show movement
- make a flip book and say how it works

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: animation · sequence · frame · flip book. This is a new unit about making pictures move. Keep it calm and one step at a time.

## What is an animation?
- it is lots of still pictures
- shown fast, one after another
- so it looks like it is moving

> 🧑‍🏫 Ask: "Can a picture move?" Take a few ideas. Then explain: a picture cannot move on its own — we show many pictures quickly so it LOOKS like it moves. Cartoons and films are made this way.

## Pictures in order
![Coloured sticky notes stacked to make a small flip book]({{res:l1-sticky-notes-for-a-flip-book-teach-computing.png}})

- each page has a small change
- in the right order they show movement

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the order task on the starter worksheet: arm down → halfway → up. Support: just find the first one. Challenge: explain why order matters.

## How to draw a flip book
- draw near the edge you flip
- start on the bottom page
- draw the part that moves first

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do → we-do. Model one page, then the next with a small change. Likely error: drawing the picture in a different place each time → fix-words "only move it a little bit."

## Your turn — make a stick person wave
- a small change on each page
- then flip it fast
- show your flip book

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: pupils make their flip book. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Support: where to draw. Challenge: why small changes work best.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Show me your flip book waving.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can explain that an animation is many pictures shown in order.
