# What's the story?

## Today we are learning
- name the setting, characters and events
- put a story in order on a storyboard
- choose a story that is simple to animate

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: setting · character · event · storyboard. Recap: last lesson we made the app take frames. Today is PLANNING — we film next lesson.

## Three story words
![A scene with two characters in front of an old building]({{res:l3-a-scene-with-a-setting-and-characters-teach-computing.png}})

- setting = where it happens
- character = who is in it
- event = what happens

> 🧑‍🏫 Point to each part of the scene. This is the sort task on the starter worksheet. Support: just find the setting. Challenge: add an event.

## A storyboard
- start → middle → end
- three simple boxes
- draw what happens in each

> 🧑‍🏫 A storyboard is a plan in pictures. This matches the order task on the activity worksheet: stand still → walk forward → wave.

## Keep it simple
- one place
- two characters
- one small event

> 🧑‍🏫 Card characters can't turn round or sit down, and each new background is extra work. Likely error: planning too much → fix-words "one place, two characters, one small event."

## Your turn — make a storyboard
- draw a start box
- draw a middle box
- draw an end box

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: pupils plan their three boxes. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Keep all sheets for next lesson. Support: order start–middle–end. Challenge: say why a busy plan is hard.
