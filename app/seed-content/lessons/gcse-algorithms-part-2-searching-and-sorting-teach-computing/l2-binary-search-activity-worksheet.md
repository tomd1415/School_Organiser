## What we are learning
Today I will carry out a binary search by checking the middle item and halving the list.

## The data
Charlie's music shop list is **in order**:

**1 Banjo · 2 Cello · 3 Drums · 4 Flute · 5 Guitar · 6 Harp · 7 Oboe · 8 Piano · 9 Violin**

A binary search looks at the **middle** item first. If that is not the one, it throws away the half that cannot contain it and checks the middle of what is left. (When there is an even number left, use the **middle-left** item.)

## Put the steps in order
Drag the steps of a binary search into the correct order.

```order
Make sure the list is in order
Look at the middle item
If it is the item you want, stop — you have found it
If your item is smaller, keep the left half
If your item is bigger, keep the right half
Find the middle of the half that is left and repeat
```

## Sort the facts
Drag each fact under the search it describes.

```sort
Binary search: needs the data in order, checks the middle item first, throws away half the data each step
Linear search: works on data in any order, checks items one by one, starts at the first item
```
