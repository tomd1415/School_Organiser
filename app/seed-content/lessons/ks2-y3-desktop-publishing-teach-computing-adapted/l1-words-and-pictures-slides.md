# Words and pictures

## Today we are learning
- the difference between text and images
- that text and images send a message
- a good point about text and about images
- how to use emojis kindly

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Same routine as every lesson. Vocabulary on the board: text · images · communicate · advantages · disadvantages. Keep the board calm — no animations.

## Text and images
- **text** = words you read
- **image** = a picture
- both can send a message

> 🧑‍🏫 We also say "writing/words" for text and "pictures/graphics" for images. In this unit we say text and images. Think-pair-share: which sends a message faster?

## Signs all around us
![A page of safety and road signs]({{res:l1-safety-and-road-signs-teach-computing.png}})

- some signs are just a picture
- some have words too

> 🧑‍🏫 Point at a few. A no-entry symbol works with no words and from far away. The DOUANE/ZOLL sign has words but you can only read it if you speak the language — so text is not always clearer. Matches the "Text or image?" sort on the worksheet.

## Good points
- **images:** no reading needed, seen from far away, work in many languages
- **text:** gives exact detail (the day, the time, a name)

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do: take "a school fair on Saturday at 2pm" — could a picture alone give the time? Lead to: words give the detail, pictures grab attention. This is the Core sort on the worksheet.

## Emojis online
- an emoji is a small image
- use them to be friendly and kind
- do not use them to be unkind

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep this short and literal. A thumbs-up to say "well done" is kind. Sending an unkind face is not okay. This is the Challenge question.
