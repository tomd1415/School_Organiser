# Great template!

## Today we are learning
- explain what page orientation means
- find placeholders and say why they help
- make a template for a magazine cover

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Same routine. Vocabulary on the board: portrait · landscape · orientation · placeholder · template · layout. Give each pupil a sheet of A4 to turn.

## A template
- a page that is already laid out
- it has spaces ready for text and pictures
- you do not start from a blank page

> 🧑‍🏫 Hold up the A4. We are making our own template today, for a magazine cover.

## Orientation: which way up?
- **portrait** = tall
- **landscape** = wide

> 🧑‍🏫 Play "show me": call portrait or landscape and pupils turn the A4. Then ask which way a magazine cover goes (portrait). This is the sort task on the worksheet.

## A magazine cover
![A magazine front cover made with desktop publishing]({{res:l3-tech-trends-magazine-cover-teach-computing.png}})

- it has a big title
- a main picture
- short, eye-catching text

> 🧑‍🏫 Editorial designers decide where the title, pictures and text go. A good cover makes people want to read it.

## Placeholders
![A magazine cover showing where the title, picture, story and quote go]({{res:l3-magazine-cover-with-placeholders-teach-computing.png}})

- a **placeholder** is a box that holds content
- one box for the title, one for a picture, one for a story

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do: point at each placeholder. This is exactly the "Find the placeholders" label task on the worksheet. Likely error: typing over the title box for everything — fix-words "one box for each thing."

## Your turn — make your template
- choose portrait
- add a box for the title, a box for a picture, a box for text

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: give them the boxes to drag in. TA: prompt, do not build it for them. We will add the real content next lesson, so keep the template.

## I can…
- tick your three "I can…"
- show me a placeholder on your template

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who placed three placeholders on their own. Save the template for Lesson 4.
