## NOT, AND, OR
- NOT gives the opposite
- AND is true only when BOTH inputs are true
- OR is true when AT LEAST ONE input is true

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do with everyday examples: "homework done AND not raining → go out". Likely error OR vs AND → "AND needs BOTH; OR needs at least one."

> 🧑‍🏫 The big picture: the processor and memory are huge collections of logic gates. Everything is switches that are on/off, 1/0.
