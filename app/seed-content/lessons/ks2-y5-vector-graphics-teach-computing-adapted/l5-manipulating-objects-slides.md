# Manipulating objects

## Today we are learning
- select more than one object at a time
- group objects so they move together
- ungroup objects to change one part
- copy and reuse a group

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: select · group · ungroup · duplicate · reuse. Drawing software open.

## Last lesson
- each object goes on a new layer
- we changed the order of layers

> 🧑‍🏫 Quick recap. Ask one pupil where a new object goes (on top).

## Select more than one
- drag a **box** around the objects
- or hold **Shift** and click each one
- now they are all selected together

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. Model the drag-box selection. All selected objects get handles.

## Group them
![A bunch of flowers made from grouped objects]({{res:l5-bouquet.png}})

- right-click and choose **Group**
- a group acts like **one** object
- move it and copy it all at once

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Group a flower, then copy it to make a bunch. The worksheet orders the group steps.

## Ungroup to change one part
![The menu showing Ungroup]({{res:l5-ungroup-menu.png}})

- right-click and choose **Ungroup**
- now change just one shape
- then **Group** it again

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: trying to recolour one part while still grouped → fix-words "ungroup first, change it, then group again." The worksheet sorts group vs ungroup jobs.

## Your turn
![A real flower photo for a background]({{res:l5-flower-photo.png}})

- make one object (a flower, a fish)
- **group** it
- copy it to build a bigger picture
- (extra: add your group onto a photo background)

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Support: group two shapes and move them. Core: group, copy, reuse. Challenge: ungroup to change one copy, then regroup, or place the group on the photo background. Nobody's screen looks less done.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Tell me one thing you grouped.
