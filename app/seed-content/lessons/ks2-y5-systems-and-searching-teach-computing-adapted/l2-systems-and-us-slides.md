# Computer systems and us

## Today we are learning
- name tasks that computer systems do for us
- sort jobs into 'person' and 'computer'
- name one good thing a computer system gives us
- keep my information safe online

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: system · sensor · person · computer · online safety.

## Starter — systems around us
- traffic lights, a bus board, a self-checkout till — all computer systems
- a paper book is NOT a computer system

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils multi-select the computer systems on the starter worksheet. Ask for one more they have used.

## A road crossing system
- a puffin crossing has a SENSOR
- the sensor sees people waiting — that is the input
- stopping the cars is the output

> 🧑‍🏫 One good thing: the cars do not get stopped when no one is really crossing. The sensor makes the system smarter.

## A shop system
![A delivery van]({{res:l2-delivery-van.png}})

- some jobs are done by a PERSON, some by a COMPUTER
- the computer checks stock, takes payment, prints the receipt
- a person packs and hands over the parcel

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils sort the jobs and order the steps on the activity worksheet. Support: sort 4. Core: order the steps. Challenge: what changes if an item is returned?

## Stay safe online
- use a password and a lock
- keep private things private: address, password, full name + school
- a search engine can store your searches

> 🧑‍🏫 Calm, factual. Link to the school's own online-safety rules. Pupils multi-select what to keep private.
