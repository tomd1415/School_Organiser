## Today we are learning
- name ways search results can be changed
- name a limit of search engines

## Starter — three people in a search
- the SEARCHER — the words you type
- the SEARCH ENGINE — its rules and its adverts
- the PAGE MAKER — the words on the page

> 🧑‍🏫 All three change what you see. Pupils sort the three actions on the starter worksheet.

## Adverts at the top
![A search with adverts]({{res:l6-sponsored-ads.png}})

- the top results say Sponsored or Ad
- these are PAID adverts
- shops pay to sit at the top

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils label the advert parts on the activity worksheet — find the "Sponsored" and the "Ad" marks. The top result is not always the best one for you.

## How search engines make money
- a search engine is free for you
- it makes money by SELLING the advert spots
- a shop pays to be seen first

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: choose how it makes money. Core: why pay to be at the top? Challenge: is the top result always the best?

## Limits of search
- a brand-new page that has not been crawled yet
- a private page that is locked
- a fact nobody has written down

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils multi-select what a search engine cannot find. Search is powerful but it is not everything.

- ranking by rules → adverts at the top
