# How search results are ranked

## Today we are learning
- put a list in rank order
- a search engine follows rules to rank pages
- name things that make a page rank higher

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: order · rank · rule · link.

## Starter — order and rank
- ranking = putting things in order, best first
- order four cities by how many people live there

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils order the cities on the starter worksheet, biggest first. Ranked order makes the biggest jump straight out at you.

## How a search engine ranks
![A search bar — 'your school']({{res:l5-google-search.png}})

- the search engine follows RULES (an algorithm)
- it gives each page a score
- the best score goes to the TOP

> 🧑‍🏫 The exact rules are a secret (we find out why next lesson). But we know some of the things that count.

## Your turn — score a page
![A school]({{res:l5-school.png}})

- make a quick paper webpage about the school
- score it: word in heading = 10, in the web address = 20, another page links to you = 50
- add up the scores and rank the pages

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: tick where the word appears. Core: add up the score. Challenge: change the page to score higher and say what changed. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## What helps a page rank?
- the search word in the heading
- the search word in the web address
- other pages link to it

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils multi-select these on the activity worksheet. A page about something totally different should NOT rank for this word.
