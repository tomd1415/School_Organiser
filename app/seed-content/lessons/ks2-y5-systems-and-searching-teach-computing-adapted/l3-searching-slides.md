# Searching the web

## Today we are learning
- use a search engine to find information
- make a search better (refine it)
- compare two search engines
- spot a website I can trust

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: search · search engine · refine · trust. Remind pupils to stay safe: use trusted sites, do not share private details.

## A search engine is a system
- input = the words you type
- output = the list of results
- examples: Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils multi-select the search engines on the starter worksheet. A calculator is not one.

## Write good search steps
- open a browser → go to a search engine → type your words → press search → read the results

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils order these steps, then write one clear search. Even if they know the answer, the steps must be clear for someone who doesn't.

## Make a search better
![Rugby]({{res:l3-rugby.png}})

- some words mean more than one thing
- Rugby = a town AND a sport; Sale = a town AND money off; Boot = a village AND footwear
- add a word to say what you mean: "Rugby town", "Boot Cumbria"

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: searching one word and stopping. Fix-words: "add a word to say exactly what you mean." This is called refining the search.

## Compare and trust
- search the same word in two search engines — are the results the same?
- a trusted site: ends in .gov.uk / .ac.uk, looks tidy, says who wrote it
- be careful of sites that promise free money

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils multi-select the signs of a trusted site. Keep this concrete with one real example on the board.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me one search that worked.
