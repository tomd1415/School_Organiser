# Wired and wireless networks

## Today we are learning
- compare wired and wireless connections
- name wireless technologies
- say what bandwidth means
- choose wired or wireless for a situation

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on board: wired · wireless · WiFi · bandwidth · bits per second · buffering.

## Starter — wired or wireless?
Look at the devices in a home: router, PC, printer, TV, console, tablet, phone. Which connect with wires, and which without?

> 🧑‍🏫 The router is always wired to the incoming line. The phone and tablet are always wireless. The rest could be either — good for discussion.

## Wired vs wireless
- Wired networks send data along cables.
- Wireless networks send data through the air using radio waves.

> 🧑‍🏫 Names of wireless technologies: Bluetooth, WiFi, 3G, 4G, 5G.

## What is bandwidth?
Bandwidth is the amount of data moved in a given time. It is measured in bits per second (Mbps, Gbps).

> 🧑‍🏫 Misconception: more bandwidth does NOT mean faster data. It's a WIDER pipe — more water at once, not faster water.

## Buffering
Buffering is when data arrives too slowly for what you are doing — the video pauses to catch up.

> 🧑‍🏫 Pipe analogy again: the pipe is too narrow for the activity. Common on a weak 3G signal.
