## The internet and the World Wide Web
![A connected world]({{res:l5-connected-world-teach-computing.png}})

- The internet = the HARDWARE (cables, routers) that connects computers.
- The World Wide Web = the web pages and links that sit ON the internet.

> 🧑‍🏫 Key point: the web is just ONE service on the internet. Tim Berners-Lee invented the web in 1989.

## Internet services
The web, email, instant messaging, VoIP, media streaming, and the Internet of Things are all services on the internet.

> 🧑‍🏫 Each service follows its own protocol (rules). For example, email uses SMTP.

## VoIP and email
- VoIP = voice over the internet (calls on WhatsApp, Skype).
- Email = messages; a lot of email is unwanted "spam".

> 🧑‍🏫 VoIP is often free if you have an internet connection. About half of all email is spam.

## The Internet of Things (IoT)
Everyday objects connected to the internet: a smart bulb, a doorbell camera, a smart speaker.

> 🧑‍🏫 Optional teacher-played hook (motion + sound — your choice): an IoT intro video — youtube.com/watch?v=ZTM9GA-4nBA (3m).

## IoT — helpful, but risky
IoT can be helpful (control things from your phone) but brings risks to PRIVACY and SECURITY — microphones and cameras can be misused if not protected.

> 🧑‍🏫 Calm, factual tone. A hacker could see if a house is empty; personal data could be shared. Both must be kept safe.
