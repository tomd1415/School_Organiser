# Technology in our classroom

## Today we are learning
- say what technology means
- sort things into technology and not technology
- find technology in the room
- say how technology helps us

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: technology. Same routine as every lesson. Keep the room calm and quiet.

## What is technology?
- technology is something PEOPLE MADE
- it helps us do something
- a computer, a pencil and scissors are all technology

> 🧑‍🏫 Common misconception: "technology must be electronic." It does not have to be — a pencil is technology too. A guide dog helps people but people did not MAKE it, so it is not technology.

## A computer is technology
![A desktop computer]({{res:l1-technology-computer.png}})

People made it. It helps us. So it is technology.

> 🧑‍🏫 Point at the parts they will name next lesson. For now, just: "this is technology because people made it to help us."

## Is it technology? — sort it
- did people make it?
- does it help us?
- if YES to both, it is technology

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the card-sort on the activity worksheet (technology / not technology). Scaffolding prompts: "Does it help us? Did people make it?" Likely error: calling a tree or a dog technology — fix-words "people did not make it." TA: prompt, do not sort it for them.

## How does it help us?
- a pencil helps us to write
- scissors help us to cut
- a lamp helps us to see in the dark

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the Core matching task. Model one: "a pencil helps us to… write." Ask pupils to use the phrase "it helps us to…".

## Technology hunt
- look around the room
- find technology
- take a photo of it

> 🧑‍🏫 Calm hunt in small groups. They photograph one piece of technology for the show-your-work box. Explorer prompt: "which of these has a computer inside?"

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me one piece of technology you found.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can say WHY something is technology (made by people + helps us). Next lesson: the parts of a computer.
