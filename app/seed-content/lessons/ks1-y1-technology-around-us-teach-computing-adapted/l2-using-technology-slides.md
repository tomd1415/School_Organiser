# Using technology

## Today we are learning
- name the main parts of a computer
- switch on and log in
- use a mouse to click and drag

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: computer · screen · base unit · keyboard · mouse · trackpad. Same routine as every lesson.

## The parts of a computer
![A desktop computer with screen, base unit, keyboard and mouse]({{res:l2-a-desktop-computer-screen-base-unit-keyboard-mouse-teach-computing.png}})

- SCREEN — you look at it
- BASE UNIT — the box that does the thinking
- KEYBOARD — you type on it
- MOUSE — you move it to point and click

> 🧑‍🏫 Point at each part as you name it. This matches the LABEL task on the activity worksheet. Have a real computer to point at if you can.

## A laptop is the same
![A laptop computer]({{res:l2-a-laptop-computer-teach-computing.png}})

- a laptop has ALL the parts in one case
- the screen, keyboard and trackpad are joined together

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils may ask why a laptop has no base unit. Fix-words: "the laptop keeps all the parts inside the case."

## Stay safe online
- today we go online
- if something online makes you feel funny or upset, tell a trusted adult

> 🧑‍🏫 First time online this unit. Recap the basic rule calmly: tell a trusted adult if anything online makes them uncomfortable. No need to dwell — keep it short and reassuring.

## Your turn — log in and use the mouse
- switch on and log in
- open the drag puzzle
- click, hold, and drag each piece

> 🧑‍🏫 Break logging in into small steps; log them in yourself if needed so everyone reaches the activity (ncce.io/drag). Model click-hold-drag. Likely error: letting go too soon — fix-words "keep the button held down while you move." TA: prompt, do not do it for them. They screenshot the puzzle.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Point to one part of the computer on your screen.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can name screen, base unit, keyboard and mouse. Log off safely at the end.
