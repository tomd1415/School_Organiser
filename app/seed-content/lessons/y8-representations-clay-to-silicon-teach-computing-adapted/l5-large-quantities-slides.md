## Starter — why different units?
We use nanometres, centimetres and kilometres. Why not just one unit?

![Large quantities]({{res:l5-large-quantities-galaxy.jpg}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Different units suit different sizes. Data is the same: bits for small, terabytes for huge.

## Bytes
1 byte = 8 bits. Bits → bytes is ÷8. Bytes → bits is ×8.

> 🧑‍🏫 Worked: 12 bytes = 96 bits. Likely error: muddles bit (b) and byte (B). Fix-words: "little b = bit, big B = byte."

## Prefixes
- kilo = thousand
- mega = million
- giga = billion
- tera = trillion

> 🧑‍🏫 Order the units smallest → largest on the worksheet. Each prefix is ×1000 of the last.

## Converting
1 KB = 1000 bytes. 16 GB = 16000 MB.

> 🧑‍🏫 Always ask "bits or bytes?" before converting. Challenge: how many 4 MB photos on a 16 GB card? (4000).

## How are bits stored? — clay to silicon
- electricity in chips
- magnetism on hard drives
- light on discs and fibre
- holes in paper (long ago)

> 🧑‍🏫 Card-sort on the worksheet. Same bit, many physical forms — that is the journey from clay to silicon.
