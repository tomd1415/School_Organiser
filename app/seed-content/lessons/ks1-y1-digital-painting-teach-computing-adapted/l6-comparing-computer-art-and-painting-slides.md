# Comparing computer art and painting

## Today we are learning
- name different ways to make a picture
- spot how a picture was made
- say what is different about computer and paper
- say which way I prefer

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: computer · paper · paint · like · prefer. Same routine.

## Lots of ways to make a picture
![A picture drawn with chalk on the ground]({{res:l6-chalk-drawing-on-the-ground-teach-computing.png}})

- chalk
- crayon
- felt tip
- paint
- a computer

> 🧑‍🏫 Be detectives: how was each picture made? Look for the clues — chalk is pastel and dusty, paint shows brush strokes, the computer has perfect neat lines.

## How can you tell?
- computer — neat lines, block colour, no brush marks
- paper — you can see chalk, crayon or brush marks

> 🧑‍🏫 The starter worksheet asks pupils to spot how a picture was made. Show examples side by side.

## I do — paint a sunflower on paper
- use real paint and a brush
- make dots like last lesson
- this time on paper, not a computer

> 🧑‍🏫 Set up the paint, paper and aprons. Remind them of their computer sunflower from last lesson. Movement break before painting.

## Computer or paper?
- on a computer you can press undo
- on paper your hands get messy
- both can make a lovely picture

> 🧑‍🏫 The activity worksheet sorts computer vs paper and rates which they preferred. There is no wrong answer — it is their choice.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Tell me which way you prefer and why.

> 🧑‍🏫 Take a class vote: computer or paper. Note who can give a reason for their choice.
