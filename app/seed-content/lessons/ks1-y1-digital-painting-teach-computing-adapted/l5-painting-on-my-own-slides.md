# Painting all by myself

## Today we are learning
- make dots of colour on the page
- change the brush colour
- change the brush size
- paint a picture on my own

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: Georges Seurat · pointillism · dots · brush size. Same routine. Today is an independent task.

## The artist — Georges Seurat
- Georges Seurat made pictures from tiny dots
- this is called pointillism
- the dots look like one picture from far away

> 🧑‍🏫 Show a Seurat painting on the board (link from the original deck — copyright, not embedded). Zoom in to show the dots.

## Painting with dots
- choose the paintbrush
- make small dots, close together
- change the colour for different parts

> 🧑‍🏫 The starter worksheet checks "pointillism = dots". Model a few dots. Likely error: dragging the brush makes a line, not dots → fix-words "tap, do not drag."

## Change the brush
- change the colour — pick a new colour
- change the size — bigger dots or smaller dots
- use undo to take away a wrong dot

> 🧑‍🏫 Model changing colour and brush size. Bigger dots are quicker; smaller dots add detail.

## I do — a dotty sunflower
- brown dots for the middle
- yellow dots for the petals
- orange dots for detail

> 🧑‍🏫 Model the sunflower. Then pupils work on their own. Support: a printed sunflower to copy. Challenge: a different picture from dots. TA: help with saving only.
