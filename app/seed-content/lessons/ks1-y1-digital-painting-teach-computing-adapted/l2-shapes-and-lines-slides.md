# Using shapes and lines

## Today we are learning
- find the square tool and the line tool
- use the shape and line tools to make a picture
- fill shapes with colour
- use undo to fix a mistake

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: square tool · line tool · fill tool · undo tool · primary colours · Piet Mondrian. Same routine.

## The artist — Piet Mondrian
- Piet Mondrian was an artist
- he made pictures from straight lines and boxes
- he used red, yellow and blue

> 🧑‍🏫 Show a Mondrian picture on the board (link from the original deck — not embedded here, it is copyright). Abstract art = shapes, lines and colours, no real object.

## The tools we need
- square tool — draws a box
- line tool — draws a straight line
- fill tool — colours a box
- undo tool — fixes a mistake

> 🧑‍🏫 Ask which tools they will need. The spray can and pencil are not needed today. The starter worksheet checks line tool and square tool.

## The primary colours
- red
- yellow
- blue

> 🧑‍🏫 Primary colours cannot be mixed from other colours. Mondrian used only these three plus white and black.

## I do — make a Mondrian picture
- draw a big square
- add straight lines to make boxes
- fill some boxes with red, yellow or blue

> 🧑‍🏫 Model it. Make a wonky line on purpose and use undo. Likely error: line not straight → fix-words "hold and drag slowly, or press undo and try again."

## Your turn
- make your own Mondrian picture
- fill some boxes, leave some white
- save your work

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Support: pre-drawn square to fill. Challenge: start from a different shape. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Screenshot onto the activity worksheet.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me one box you filled.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can find the square and line tools and use fill and undo.
