# Why did I choose that?

## Today we are learning
- say what each paint tool does
- choose the best tool for the job
- pick colours for my picture
- say which tools helped me

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: tool · choose · colour · feeling · Wassily Kandinsky. Same routine.

## Each tool has a job
![A paint pot and a paintbrush]({{res:l1-paint-tools.png}})

- paintbrush — a soft line
- fill — colours a whole shape
- eraser — rubs out
- undo — goes back a step

> 🧑‍🏫 Quick-fire: hold up the question, pupils raise a hand for A, B or C. The starter worksheet matches each tool to its job.

## The best tool for the job
- to colour a whole shape → fill
- to make a neat circle → shape tool
- to fix a mistake → undo

> 🧑‍🏫 Stress: choosing the RIGHT tool makes the job easier. Likely error: using the eraser to change a colour → fix-words "to change a colour, use undo or fill, not the eraser."

## The artist — Wassily Kandinsky
- Kandinsky made "Squares with Concentric Circles"
- it is made of squares with circles inside
- he chose colours to show feelings

> 🧑‍🏫 Show the Kandinsky picture on the board (link from the original deck — copyright, not embedded). Bright colours can feel happy.

## I do — circles in squares
- make a grid of squares
- draw circles inside, biggest first
- fill with bright colours

> 🧑‍🏫 Model choosing the shape tool then the fill tool, saying why each time. You-do: pupils make their own. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Screenshot onto the worksheet.
