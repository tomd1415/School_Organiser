# Drawing designs

## Today we are learning
- identify a real-world example of a condition starting an action
- describe what my project will do
- create a detailed, labelled drawing of my project

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: selection · condition · action · design · input · output · wiring diagram. Same routine.

## Selection all around us
- lights that come on when it gets dark
- doors that open when someone approaches
- taps that run when hands go under them

> 🧑‍🏫 Each one is "if a condition, then an action". This matches the match task on the starter worksheet.

## The project
![A fairground carousel]({{res:l5-carousel-photo.jpg}})

The task: build a carousel that uses **selection** to start when a switch is pressed, with at least **two output devices**.

> 🧑‍🏫 Name the ride (a "swing ride" / "chair-o-plane"). Keep it concrete — show the real photo.

## A model carousel
![A cardboard carousel model with a Crumble]({{res:l5-carousel-model.jpg}})

- a cardboard tower, a motor on top, a Crumble and a switch
- the switch is the **input**; the motor and Sparkle are the **outputs**

> 🧑‍🏫 Pass the demonstration model round if it suits. Ask: where is the input? where are the outputs?

## Design your model
- describe what it will do
- choose your input and outputs
- draw a labelled diagram and a wiring plan

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: forgetting the input. Fix-words: "selection needs a condition — what is the input?" Support: tick the parts. Challenge: write the if…then.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Show a partner your design.

> 🧑‍🏫 You keep these designs and models for Lesson 6. Note who has a clear input and two outputs.
