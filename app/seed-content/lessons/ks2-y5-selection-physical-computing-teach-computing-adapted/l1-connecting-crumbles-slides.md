# Connecting Crumbles

## Today we are learning
- build a simple circuit and connect it to a microcontroller
- program a microcontroller to make an LED switch on
- explain what an infinite loop does

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: microcontroller · Crumble · Sparkle (an LED) · component · connect · infinite loop. Same routine as every lesson.

## Meet the Crumble
![The Crumble controller board]({{res:l1-crumble-board.png}})

- a **microcontroller** is a small computer we can program
- the Crumble has a USB port, power pads, A B C D pads and motor outputs 1 and 2

> 🧑‍🏫 Point at each part: USB port (to the computer), the power pads, the A–D pads, the motor outputs. This matches the label task on the starter worksheet.

## A Sparkle is an LED
- a **Sparkle** is a small light (an LED) that can be any colour
- it connects to the power pads and to the D pad

> 🧑‍🏫 Hold up a Sparkle. Keep it concrete — pass one round if that suits the class.

## Connect the circuit
- battery box → power pads
- Sparkle + and – → power pads, Sparkle D → D pad
- switch on — the Sparkle flashes white 6 times when it is right

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: a + lead on a – pad. Fix-words: "match plus to plus, minus to minus." The white flash is the "it works" signal.

## Program the Sparkle
- drag blocks to turn the Sparkle on, wait, then off
- click the green play button to send it to the Crumble

> 🧑‍🏫 Demonstrate live: set sparkle → wait → turn off. Let pupils copy block by block (I-do / we-do).

## Infinite loop
![A do forever program]({{res:l1-infinite-loop-code.png}})

- a **do forever** loop repeats the blocks inside it again and again
- this is called an **infinite loop** — it never stops on its own

> 🧑‍🏫 "Infinite" means it goes on for ever. Support: pair them up. Challenge: change colours and timings.
