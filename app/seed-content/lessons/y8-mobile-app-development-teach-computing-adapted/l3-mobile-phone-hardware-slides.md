## Inside a phone
A phone is full of hardware: CPU, RAM, battery, screen, speaker and sensors.

![The parts on a circuit board]({{res:l3-circuit-board-teach-computing.jpg}})

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils label this board on the activity worksheet. Likely error: mixes up CPU and RAM → fix-words "CPU thinks, RAM remembers (for now)."

## Sensors
GPS finds location · accelerometer detects movement · gyroscope senses rotation · proximity sensor detects when the phone is near your ear.

> 🧑‍🏫 Match the sensor to its job on the worksheet. Use real examples: step counting, screen rotation, sat-nav.

## Case study — what hardware does the app need?
A racing game uses the accelerometer and gyroscope. A fitness app uses GPS and the accelerometer.

> 🧑‍🏫 Pairs discuss the hardware for each app, then sort the components into groups on the worksheet.

## Limitations
Hardware has limits — for example the **battery**. GPS and the screen use a lot of power.

> 🧑‍🏫 Link to their own app design: which hardware will it need, and what is the cost (battery, privacy)?
