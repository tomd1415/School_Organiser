# App development (part 2)

## Today we are learning
- give helpful feedback on a classmate's app
- add a second screen to my app
- add a button that links between two screens
- write a simple test plan

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Same routine as every lesson.

## Peer review
Look at a classmate's app. Say what works, then suggest one improvement.

> 🧑‍🏫 Model kind, specific feedback. Pupils sort helpful vs unhelpful comments on the worksheet. Keep it kind — feedback is about the app, not the person.

## Tutorial — add a second screen
![The App Lab Design view]({{res:l6-app-lab-design-view-teach-computing.png}})

Add screen2, then a button on screen1 that goes to it.

> 🧑‍🏫 Demonstrate slowly (I do). Pupils follow the "add a second screen" steps on the worksheet. Likely error: button does nothing → fix-words "the button needs an onEvent."

## Develop your app
Add your second screen and link the screens. Use your wireframe.

> 🧑‍🏫 You do. Support: follow the ordered steps. Challenge: add extra functionality beyond navigation.

## Testing and test plans
A test plan says what you will do and what should happen.

> 🧑‍🏫 Discuss why testing matters — bugs annoy users. Pupils write one test on the worksheet (do → should happen).

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me your app with two screens.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary: pupils paste their App Lab link + a screenshot and rate their confidence. Note who linked two screens.
