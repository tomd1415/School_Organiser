# App development (part 1)

## Today we are learning
- find my way around the App Lab Design view
- add elements (button, label, image) to a screen
- build the welcome screen of my app
- explain what selection (if) does in an app

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Same routine as every lesson.

## Starter — meet App Lab
App Lab is where we build the app. It has Code, Design and Data views.

> 🧑‍🏫 Optional: play the short App Lab intro clip (teacher-played hook — has motion and sound, preview first). Then pupils answer the starter.

## The App Lab Design view
![The App Lab Design view]({{res:l5-app-lab-interface.png}})

Design tab · Toolbox · Phone screen · Properties · Run.

> 🧑‍🏫 Demonstrate slowly (I do). Pupils label this screen on the worksheet. Likely error: edits in the wrong panel → fix-words "Design to add, Properties to change."

## Build your welcome screen
Drag a Label and a Button. Change the text in Properties. Press Run.

> 🧑‍🏫 We do one together, then you do. Support: follow the "add a button" steps on the worksheet. Refer back to their wireframe.

## Selection — an "if"
Selection lets the app choose: **if** the user taps Start, **then** show the next screen.

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep it concrete. Challenge: pupils write an "if" example for their own app.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show me your welcome screen.

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils paste their App Lab share link + a screenshot. Note who built a working welcome screen.
