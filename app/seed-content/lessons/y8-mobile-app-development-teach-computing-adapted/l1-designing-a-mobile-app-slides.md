# Designing a mobile app

## Today we are learning
- describe the Health and Fitness Tracker app and who it is for
- list features that make an app user-friendly
- explain what user-centred design means
- draw a wireframe for one screen

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Same routine as every lesson.

## Starter — how can apps help health and fitness?
On your starter worksheet, tick all the things a health app could track.

> 🧑‍🏫 Take answers: steps, sleep, heart rate, nutrition. Be sensitive — some pupils use these apps and may not want to share. Keep it general; never single anyone out.

## Our app — the Health and Fitness Tracker
A new app to help people set and reach health and fitness goals.

> 🧑‍🏫 Target audience = who the app is for. Keep it cohort-level; do not describe any one pupil's health.

## What makes an app user-friendly?
Easy to use · works on all devices · clear design · help is available.

> 🧑‍🏫 Discuss in pairs, then sort the cards on the activity worksheet. Likely error: "user-friendly = looks pretty" → fix-words "user-friendly = easy to USE."

## User-centred design
We design the app around the **user** — what they need and find easy.

> 🧑‍🏫 Ask which apps they use that feel easy. Support: word-choice on the worksheet. Challenge: who is the target user?

## What is a wireframe?
A wireframe is a simple sketch of one screen — boxes for buttons, text and pictures.
