## Starter — three big ideas
Three pictures, three ideas: encryption · password security · data privacy.

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils name the idea on the starter worksheet. Take answers and explain each one simply.

## Encryption
Encryption scrambles data with a key. Without the key it cannot be read.

> 🧑‍🏫 Analogy: a locked box — only the right key opens it. Used in messaging apps (more soon).

## Password security
A strong password uses upper and lower case, numbers, symbols, or three random words.

> 🧑‍🏫 Stress: a different password for each account. Two-step login adds "something you have" (your phone). Likely error: "longer is enough" → fix-words "long AND mixed AND different."

## Data privacy
Your personal data should be kept private and protected. A law called GDPR sets the rules.

> 🧑‍🏫 Ask where their data is stored (school, doctor, shops). Keep it general and cohort-level.
