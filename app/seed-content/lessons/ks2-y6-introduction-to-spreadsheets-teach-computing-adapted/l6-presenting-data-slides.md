# Presenting data

## Today we are learning
- make a chart
- use a chart to answer questions
- choose when to use a table or a chart

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: chart · pie chart · table · compare. End-of-unit lesson — recap the whole journey at the end.

## Starter — a chart is a picture of data
![A pie chart of party costs]({{res:l6-pie-chart.png}})

- a chart turns numbers into a picture
- a pie chart shows the parts of a whole
- the biggest slice is the biggest amount

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils read the biggest slice on the starter worksheet. Support pupils choose what a chart is.

## Making a chart
- highlight the cells you want
- click Insert, then Chart
- choose the chart type (e.g. pie chart)

> 🧑‍🏫 Model making a pie chart from the party data. Pupils order the steps, then make their own and screenshot it. Likely error: highlighting the wrong cells. Fix-words: "highlight only the totals you want to compare."

## Reading a chart
![A party spreadsheet with a chart]({{res:l6-party-table.png}})

- find the biggest and smallest slices
- compare two amounts
- use it to answer a question

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils read venue vs drinks on the activity worksheet. TA: prompt them to point at the slice, do not answer for them.

## Table or chart?
- table: every exact figure; doing calculations
- chart: comparing amounts; a picture to share

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils sort jobs into table / chart. Challenge: detailed netball stats → a table is better. Each pupil gives one reason to use a chart.

## End of unit — I can…
- collect data → format it → formulas → calculate → plan an event → present it
- tick your three "I can…" and rate your confidence

> 🧑‍🏫 Recap the six lessons as one journey. Exit ticket: one thing a spreadsheet is good for. Movement break to finish.
