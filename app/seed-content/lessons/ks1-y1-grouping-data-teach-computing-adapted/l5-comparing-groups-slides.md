# Comparing groups

## Today we are learning
- choose how to group objects
- describe groups of objects
- record how many objects are in a group

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: group · property · more · less · most · fewest. Same routine as every lesson.

## Comparing words
- COMPARING means looking at what is the same and what is different
- more · less · most · fewest · the same

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do: model each comparing word with two real groups on the table.

## Choose how to group
- YOU choose the property (colour, shape or size)
- drag the objects into your groups

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do: pupils use their own copy of the "Creating groups" activity, or the card sort on the activity worksheet. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Record how many
- count each group
- write the number next to each group

> 🧑‍🏫 Whole numbers only. Likely error: counting an object twice — fix-words "touch each one once."

## Describe your groups
- use a comparing word: more · fewer · the most · the fewest
- "Group A has more than Group B"

> 🧑‍🏫 Hand out the "Vocabulary for describing" support words. Challenge: compare three groups, not two.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Tell me which of your groups has the most.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can record the count AND describe the groups with a comparing word.
