# Answering questions

## Today we are learning
- decide how to group objects to answer a question
- compare groups of objects
- record and share what I have found

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: group · property · compare · most · fewest · the same. Same routine as every lesson.

## Starter — true or false?
![A red car]({{res:l6-red-car.png}})
![A cat]({{res:l6-cat.png}})

- thumbs up for true, thumbs down for false
- "these are grouped by colour" — true or false?

> 🧑‍🏫 Run a few true/false statements (grouped by colour? by shape? which group has more?). Ask pupils to explain their reasoning before you reveal.

## A question to answer
- "How many cats are there?"
- you can answer it by GROUPING the objects

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1: show a big mixed pile. Ask the question. Let them feel that counting a mess is slow — last lesson's idea.

## Group to answer — keep it simple
![A cat]({{res:l6-cat.png}})

- you only need TWO groups: "cat" and "not cat"
- you do not need to sort everything else

> 🧑‍🏫 Big idea: the quickest way to answer is the SMALLEST number of groups that answers the question.

## Count and compare
- count the "cat" group — that is your answer
- compare: which group has more, "cat" or "not cat"?

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do: count the target group, then compare the two groups with a comparing word.

## Record and share
- write down your answer
- tell a partner what you found and how you grouped

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do on the activity worksheet (order → sort → record), then real objects. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Each pupil shares one finding.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Tell a partner one thing you found out.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can choose two helpful groups, answer the question, and share what they found. End of unit — recap the journey: label → group → count → compare → answer questions.
