# Group and count

## Today we are learning
- count objects
- group objects
- count a group of objects

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: group · object · label · image. Same routine as every lesson.

- count them out loud together
- point to each one as you count

> 🧑‍🏫 No animation/sound version: reveal the objects on paper or the board and count by pointing. One-to-one counting.

## Counting a messy pile is hard
- when objects are mixed up it is easy to lose count
- you forget what you have already counted

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1 (physical): scatter 10–20 real objects, NO moving allowed. Let them feel that counting a mess is hard. This is the point of the lesson.

## Make it easier — put them in groups
![A blue car]({{res:l2-blue-car-teach-computing.png}})

- sort the objects into groups first
- then count one group at a time

> 🧑‍🏫 Think-pair-share: "How could you make counting easier?" Steer to: group them. Then they may move the objects.

## Count each group
- count the first group, write the number
- count the next group, write the number

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do then you-do the card sort + count on the activity worksheet. TA: prompt, do not count it for them.

## Computers are not clever
- a computer can only "count" pictures a person has grouped and labelled
- the person does the thinking; the computer does the counting

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary idea: link back to last lesson — a person labels, then the computer can count the group.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Tell me how many are in one of your groups.

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can group first and then count each group.
