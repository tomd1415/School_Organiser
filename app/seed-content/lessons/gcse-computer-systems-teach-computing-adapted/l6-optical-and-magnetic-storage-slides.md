# Optical and magnetic storage

## Today we are learning
- explain how optical storage stores data
- explain how magnetic storage stores data
- compare the three types of storage

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: optical · laser · pit · land · magnetic · magnetised · platter. Same routine as every lesson.

## Starter — data is binary
- every device stores data as 1s and 0s
- text uses ASCII (each letter has a number)

> 🧑‍🏫 Short ASCII decode on the starter worksheet. Keep it light: the point is "it all becomes binary before it is stored."

## Optical storage
![The laser lens inside an optical drive]({{res:l6-the-laser-lens-inside-an-optical-drive-teach-computing.jpg}})

- CD, DVD, Blu-ray
- a LASER reads the surface
- tiny PITS and LANDS stand for 1s and 0s

> 🧑‍🏫 "Optical" = light. Likely error: "the data is printed on the disc like a photo" → fix-words "the laser reads bumps (pits) and flats (lands)."

## Magnetic storage
![An open hard disk drive]({{res:l6-open-hard-disk-drive-platter-and-read-write-arm-teach-computing.png}})

- a hard disk drive (HDD)
- a spinning PLATTER, read by a READ/WRITE ARM
- magnetised area = 1, demagnetised area = 0

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the diagram for the LABEL task: platter, read/write arm, casing. Point out the moving parts — that is why it is slower and easier to damage than an SSD.

## Compare the three types
- SOLID STATE — fastest, no moving parts, costs most per GB
- OPTICAL — cheap to post, easy to scratch
- MAGNETIC — huge capacity for a low price, has moving parts

> 🧑‍🏫 This sets up the compare sort task. Pupils will use these comparisons next lesson to choose a device.
