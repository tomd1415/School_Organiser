## Starter — why not just RAM?
- RAM is volatile — it loses everything when the power is off
- so we need storage that KEEPS our files: secondary storage

> 🧑‍🏫 "Why don't we store everything in RAM?" Pull out: volatile, not enough space, can't carry it to another computer. Matches the starter worksheet.

## How solid-state storage works
- NO moving parts
- stores data in tiny electronic circuits (flash memory)
- fast, light and durable

> 🧑‍🏫 No moving parts = fast and hard to break. Likely error: "an SSD spins like a CD" → fix-words "solid state has NO spinning parts at all."

## Advantages and disadvantages
- ✓ fast, ✓ light, ✓ durable, ✓ silent
- ✗ costs more per gigabyte, ✗ can wear out after very many writes

> 🧑‍🏫 Relate to cost: a laptop with an SSD costs more than one with a hard drive. Mention SSDs can wear out after huge numbers of writes.
