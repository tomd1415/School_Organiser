## Meet John von Neumann
![John von Neumann]({{res:l2-john-von-neumann-teach-computing.png}})

- a brilliant scientist (maths, physics, computing)
- his big idea: store the PROGRAM and the DATA together in memory

> 🧑‍🏫 He was a polymath — good at many fields. His design is the basis of nearly all modern computers.

## Before von Neumann
![A room-sized early computer (ENIAC)]({{res:l2-eniac-a-room-sized-early-computer-teach-computing.png}})

- old computers were programmed with physical switches and wires
- changing the program could take days or weeks

> 🧑‍🏫 Use this to show WHY storing the program in memory was such a leap — you just load a new program instead of rewiring.

## Inside the CPU
![A diagram of the parts inside the CPU]({{res:l2-the-parts-inside-the-cpu-teach-computing.png}})

- ALU = does the maths and logic
- control unit = tells the other parts what to do
- registers = tiny, very fast stores
- clock = sets the speed (pulses per second)

> 🧑‍🏫 This is the diagram for the sort task. Likely error: "the ALU is in charge" → fix-words "the CONTROL UNIT is in charge; the ALU just does the sums."

## Buses
- buses are sets of wires that carry data and signals between parts
- they connect the CPU to memory

> 🧑‍🏫 A bus is a "road" for data. Keep it simple at this stage; the FDE lesson next will use the buses.
