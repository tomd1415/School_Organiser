# Selecting a storage device

## Today we are learning
- recommend a storage device for a situation and give a reason
- describe the limits of secondary storage
- explain what cloud storage is

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: capacity · portable · durable · cost · cloud storage. Same routine as every lesson.

## Starter — the impact of data loss
- losing personal data can harm real people
- stolen data can be used for fraud

> 🧑‍🏫 Use a news headline about a data breach. Pull out the ethical and legal impacts. This links to cloud storage later (you trust a company with your data).

## How to choose a device
- read the situation and find the CLUES
- capacity (how much?), portable (does it travel?), durable (tough?), cost (budget?)

> 🧑‍🏫 Model this fully with Scenario 1 (Jamie) before pupils try Scenario 2 (Maja). Highlight the clue words in each description.

## Scenario 1 — Jamie
- posts wedding photos (about 2 GB) to his mum
- needs: portable, durable, enough capacity → a USB stick or a DVD

> 🧑‍🏫 Model the thinking out loud. Likely error: "use the internal hard drive" → fix-words "it has to be POSTED, so it must be portable." Matches the worksheet.

## Scenario 2 — Maja
- sends a video game to thousands of paying customers
- needs cheap copies to post in bulk → DVDs (optical)

> 🧑‍🏫 Step back your modelling here — let pupils pick out the clues. Bring out cost-per-copy for mass distribution.

## Cloud storage
![Cloud storage in a data centre]({{res:l7-cloud.png}})

- your files kept on someone else's servers, over the internet
- ✓ reach them anywhere, easy to share
- ✗ needs internet, you must trust the company

> 🧑‍🏫 "Cloud" just means other people's computers in a data centre. Connect the disadvantages back to the starter (trusting a company with your data).
