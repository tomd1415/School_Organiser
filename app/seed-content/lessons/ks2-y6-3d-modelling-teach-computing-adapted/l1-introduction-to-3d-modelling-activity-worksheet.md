## What we are learning
Today I will add 3D shapes in Tinkercad, look at them from different sides, and move them next to each other.

## The Tinkercad screen
Tinkercad is the program we use to make 3D models. Three parts you will use a lot:
- the **workplane** — the blue grid where you build your model.
- the **shapes** — the panel of ready-made shapes you drag on.
- the **view tools** — the buttons that turn and zoom your view.

![The Tinkercad screen]({{res:l1-tinkercad-interface.png}})

## Looking from different sides (perspective)
The way something looks changes when you move around it. This is called **perspective**. The shapes do not move — only your view moves.

![The same two shapes seen from the top]({{res:l1-perspective-top.png}})

## Moving a shape
Put the steps in order to move a shape on the workplane.

```order
Click on the shape to select it
The shape turns blue and shows handles
Click and drag the shape to a new place
Let go of the mouse to drop it
```
