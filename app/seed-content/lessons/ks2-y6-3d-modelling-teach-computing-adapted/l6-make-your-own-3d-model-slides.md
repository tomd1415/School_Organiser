# Make your own 3D model

## Today we are learning
- to construct a 3D model based on our design
- to evaluate our model and a partner's model
- to explain how our model could be improved
- to modify our model to improve it

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: construct · evaluate · modify. This is the last lesson — recap the whole unit at the end.

## From plan to model
![A house pulled apart into its shapes]({{res:l6-a-house-pulled-apart-into-its-shapes-teach-computing.png}})

- use your plan from last lesson
- think which techniques you will need
- moving, resizing, lifting, duplicating, grouping

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter: pupils tick the techniques they will use. Remind them all of these skills come from earlier lessons.

## Build it in Tinkercad
![An example model built in Tinkercad]({{res:l6-an-example-3d-model-built-in-tinkercad-teach-computing.png}})

- build your shapes one at a time
- follow your plan
- save and screenshot when you are done

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do (longest activity). Keep instructions brief so pupils work independently. TA: prompt with the matching skill, do not build it for them.

## Evaluate with a partner
- evaluate means judge how good your work is
- are the parts the right size next to each other (in proportion)?
- what extra detail could you add?

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils rate their own model (1–5) and give a partner one kind idea. Likely error: "evaluate means it is bad." Fix-words: "evaluate means spot what is GOOD and what to improve."

## Improve your model
- pick one or two improvements
- make the changes
- take a new screenshot to show the change

> 🧑‍🏫 Challenge: explain why the change makes the model better. Support choose what evaluate means.

## End of the unit
- 2D and 3D → adding and moving shapes → resizing, lifting, recolouring
- rotating, duplicating, grouping → a name badge → a desk tidy
- planning like an architect → building your own model

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Ask: what was easy, what was tricky, when is 3D modelling helpful? Pupils self-tick the four "I can…".
