## What we are learning
Today I will insert an item into an ordered list, then carry out a full insertion sort.

## Inserting one card
An insertion sort builds a **sorted part** at the start of the list. It takes the next item from the **unsorted part** and slides it into the right place.

![Inserting a card into a sorted row: the bigger sorted cards slide right to make a gap, and the new card drops into it]({{res:l8-insert-a-card-teach-computing.png}})

## Put the steps in order
Drag the steps for inserting one item into order.

```order
Take the next item from the unsorted part
Compare it with the items already sorted
Move the bigger sorted items one place to the right
Keep going until you find the gap
Put the item into the gap
```

## Sort the facts
Drag each fact under the algorithm it describes.

```sort
Insertion sort: builds a sorted part at the start, inserts each item into its place, slides bigger items to the right
Bubble sort: compares two neighbours at a time, swaps any out-of-order pair, the biggest item ends each pass at the end
```
