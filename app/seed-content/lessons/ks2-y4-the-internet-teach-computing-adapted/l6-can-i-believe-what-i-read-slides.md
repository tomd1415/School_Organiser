# Can I believe what I read?

## Today we are learning
- explain that not everything on the web is true
- explain why some information online may not be honest or accurate
- explain why I need to think before I share or reshare content

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: information · true · false · accurate · honest · share. Same routine as every lesson. Keep this lesson calm and at the level of the whole class — no personal online stories.

## Is it real?
![A budgie with a tiger's head — an edited, fake picture]({{res:l6-an-edited-fake-animal-picture-teach-computing.png}})

- some pictures are **edited** (changed on a computer)
- this animal is not real — two pictures joined together

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter: vote real / not real, then reveal it is edited. Links to the Year 4 photo-editing unit. Fix-words: "if it looks impossible, it may be edited."

## Why is some information not true?
![Money — a reason false information is shared]({{res:l6-money-a-reason-false-information-is-shared-teach-computing.png}})

- **to make money** — false claims to sell something
- **to be popular** — untrue posts to get likes
- **by mistake** — sharing without knowing it is untrue

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep it factual and brief. The point: information online can be wrong, and there are reasons why people put it there.

## How information spreads
- one person shares → friends share → many people share
- once it has spread, you **cannot take it back**

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do the `order` task. Big idea: sharing is fast and hard to undo. This is why we think first.

## Think before you share
![Sharing spreads fast on social media]({{res:l6-sharing-spreads-fast-on-social-media-teach-computing.png}})

- check if it is **true**
- think about **who made it**
- if you are not sure, **ask a trusted adult**

> 🧑‍🏫 Challenge multi-select reinforces the checks. Keep cohort-level: this is a habit for everyone, not a story about any one pupil.

## I can…
Tick your "I can…". Tell me one thing to check before you share.

> 🧑‍🏫 Check pupils can say "not everything online is true" and name one check. End of unit: recap the journey — networks → the internet → the World Wide Web → owning and trusting content.
