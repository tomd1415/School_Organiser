# How we communicate

## Today we are learning
- different ways people communicate
- ways to communicate over the internet
- how to choose a way that suits a job

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: communicate · message · internet · purpose. Recap: the internet connects people far apart.

## Starter — so many ways to say "Yes"
- nod, thumbs up, say the word, write it down
- some ways only work face to face

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils write two non-speaking ways to say "Yes" on the starter worksheet. Then ask which ones fail when you are far apart.

## When we are far apart
![The internet connects people around the world]({{res:l5-the-internet-connects-people-teach-computing.png}})

- a nod or a thumbs up needs you to be seen
- the internet lets us communicate over any distance

> 🧑‍🏫 Support pupils choose which way works far away. Challenge: why does a thumbs up not work to another town?

## Communicating over the internet
- a message, a video call, an email, a shared post
- different ways suit different jobs

> 🧑‍🏫 Build a class list on the board. Pupils tick the internet ways on the activity worksheet (shouting across the playground is the odd one out).

## Your turn — choose the best way
- quick notice to the whole class → a message to everyone
- talk face to face to family abroad → a video call
- send your finished report → an email

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils match each job to the best way on the activity worksheet. Then make a mind map for their scenario — keep it for next lesson.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Tell me one way to communicate over the internet.

> 🧑‍🏫 Listen for a sensible match of way-to-job. Movement break before the next lesson.
