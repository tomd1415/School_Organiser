# Data packets

## Today we are learning
- data is sent over networks in small parts called packets
- a packet has two main parts: the header and the data payload
- all data — text, pictures, video and sound — is sent in packets

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: packet · header · data payload. Recap last lesson: every device has an IP address.

## Starter — big data, small parts
![The internet connects computers around the world]({{res:l2-the-internet-a-global-network-teach-computing.png}})

- a computer breaks data into small parts to send it
- each small part is a packet
- small parts are quicker and easier to send

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils choose what a packet is on the starter worksheet. Core: why break data into small parts?

## The two parts of a packet
- HEADER = the to address, the from address, the packet number, total packets
- DATA PAYLOAD = the actual data (the message)
- like a letter in an envelope: the envelope is the header, the letter inside is the payload

> 🧑‍🏫 Likely error: "the header is the message." Fix-words: "the header is the ENVELOPE (the addresses); the payload is the LETTER inside." Pupils sort the parts on the activity worksheet.

## Packets are numbered
- a message is split into several packets
- each packet has a number, and the total number of packets
- the receiving computer uses the numbers to put them back in order

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils order the three packets on the activity worksheet to read "data is transferred in packets". Challenge: how does a computer know a packet is missing?

## All data travels in packets
- not just text — pictures, video and sound too
- everything sent on the internet is broken into packets

> 🧑‍🏫 A photo would really be thousands of tiny packets, not three. Support choose, Core/Challenge explain.
