# Shared working

## Today we are learning
- different ways of working together online
- working online can be public or private
- how to reuse and remix work, and when it is OK

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Words on the board: reuse · remix · public · private · copyright. Recap: last lesson we made something together online.

## Starter — build on a drawing
- draw a simple picture, put your name on it
- pass it to a partner — they add to it
- you have reused and changed someone's work

> 🧑‍🏫 Three minutes drawing, then swap. Keep names on so drawings go back. This is the hands-on way into "remix".

## Remixing in Scratch
![The Mars Lander project with See inside and Remix buttons]({{res:l4-mars-lander-see-inside-and-remix-teach-computing.png}})

- See inside = look at the blocks
- Remix = make your own copy to change
- one starter project → a different version for everyone

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils need to be logged in to Scratch to save a remix. Likely error: "remix changes the original." Fix-words: "remix makes your OWN copy." Pupils order the remix steps on the worksheet.

## Your turn — remix the Mars Lander
- open the project, click See inside, then Remix
- change the sprite, colours, sounds or blocks
- make it your own

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: choose what Remix does. Core: tick what you changed. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Movement break before the plenary.

## Is it always OK?
- working online can be public or private
- you need permission, and you should say whose work it was
- this is copyright

> 🧑‍🏫 "It's always OK to use someone's work" — true or false? It is false. Draw out permission + crediting.
