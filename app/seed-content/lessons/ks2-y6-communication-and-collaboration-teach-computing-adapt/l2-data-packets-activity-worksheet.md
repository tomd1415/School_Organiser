## The two parts of a packet
Every packet has two main parts:
- the **header** — the information that gets the packet to the right place: the **to** address, the **from** address, the **packet number**, and how many packets there are in total.
- the **data payload** — the actual data being sent (the message, or part of a picture).

A packet is like a letter in an envelope. The **envelope** (with the address on it) is the header. The **letter inside** is the data payload.
