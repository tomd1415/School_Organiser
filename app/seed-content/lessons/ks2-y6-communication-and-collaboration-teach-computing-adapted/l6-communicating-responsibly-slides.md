# Communicating responsibly

## Today we are learning
- compare different ways of communicating on the internet
- decide when I should and should not share information online
- communication online may not be private
- what to do if I see something upsetting online

## Comparing ways to communicate
- public or private — who can see it?
- one-to-one or one-to-many — one person or many?

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils sort ways into public/private and one-to-one/one-to-many on the activity worksheet. Use familiar examples: a text vs a public video.

## Think before you share
- online messages are not always private
- keep private: your address, your password, your full name and school
- think before you post — it can be hard to take back

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils tick what to keep private. Draw out that "private" settings can change and screenshots can be shared on.

## If you see something upsetting
- stop — do not share it
- turn off or minimise the screen
- tell a trusted adult straight away
- together, report it

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils order the safety steps on the activity worksheet. Link to your school's online-safety rules (e.g. SMART). Keep it calm and matter-of-fact.
