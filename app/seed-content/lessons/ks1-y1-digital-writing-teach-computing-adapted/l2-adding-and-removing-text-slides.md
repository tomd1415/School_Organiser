# Adding and removing text

## Today we are learning
- type letters and words
- use the Space key to make gaps
- use Backspace to remove text

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: space · backspace · text cursor · enter. Same routine as last lesson.

## Starter — catdog
![A computer keyboard]({{res:l2-computer-keyboard-teach-computing.png}})

- "catdog" has no gap
- we need the Space key

> 🧑‍🏫 Recap the keyboard from Lesson 1. Think, pair, share: how do we put a gap between two words? The Space key is the long key at the bottom.

## The text cursor
- the flashing line is the text cursor
- it shows where your letters will appear
- click with the mouse to move it

> 🧑‍🏫 Model moving the cursor with the mouse, then typing. Likely error: typing in the wrong place — fix-words "click first, then type."

## Backspace fixes a mistake
- Backspace removes the letter on the left
- press it once for one letter

> 🧑‍🏫 Demonstrate making a mistake on purpose and using Backspace. This is the AP "Using Backspace" idea. No saving needed today.

## Your turn — describe the lost toy
- type a short sentence about the lost toy
- use the Space key between words
- use Backspace to fix any mistakes

> 🧑‍🏫 Sentence starters on the board. Support: tell them the area of the keyboard a key is in. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Never type it for them.

## I can…
- tick your three "I can…"
- show me a space and a Backspace fix

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can use Space and Backspace on their own.
