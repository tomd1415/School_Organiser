# Pencil or keyboard?

## Today we are learning
- make changes to text on a computer
- say how typing and paper writing are the same and different
- say which one I like best and why

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: compare · typing · writing. Same routine. Open a blank document.

## Two ways to write

![A computer keyboard]({{res:l6-computer-keyboard-teach-computing.png}})

- one way: a pencil on paper
- another way: keys on a computer

> 🧑‍🏫 Show both. This matches the "paper or computer" sort on the starter worksheet.

## Fixing a mistake
- on paper: you have to cross it out or rub it out
- on a computer: Backspace removes it cleanly

> 🧑‍🏫 Before the lesson, write a misspelt sentence on a flip chart so pupils see how hard it is to undo pen. On the computer, model fixing "brownfur" → "brown fur" with Backspace and the Space key.

## Same and different
- SAME: we use spaces between words both ways
- DIFFERENT: paper uses a pencil; a computer uses keys

> 🧑‍🏫 Build two lists with the class: same / different. Give one example each to start them off. Keep it short and calm.

## Which do you prefer?
- "I like writing on…"
- "…because…"

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary: pupils choose and give a reason. Both answers are fine — we want the reason. They record this on the activity worksheet (the sentence and the 1–5 scale).

## I can…
- tick your three "I can…"
- tell me which you prefer and why

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can give a reason for their choice. End-of-unit: recap the journey — keyboard → adding/removing text → toolbar → changing text → comparing.
