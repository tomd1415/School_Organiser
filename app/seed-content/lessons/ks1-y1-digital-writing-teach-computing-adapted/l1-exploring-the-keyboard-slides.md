# Exploring the keyboard

## Today we are learning
- open a word processor
- find keys on a keyboard
- type my name

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Same routine as every lesson. Vocabulary on the board: word processor · keyboard · keys · letters · type.

## Starter — where can we write?
- a pencil, chalk, a pen…
- and a computer

> 🧑‍🏫 Think, pair, share: where could you write text? Lead to the answer "a computer". Keep it calm and short.

## A word processor
- a word processor is a page on the computer that you can write on
- it is like a blank piece of paper

> 🧑‍🏫 Hold up a blank sheet of paper next to the blank document. Both are pages you can write on. No sound or animation on the board.

## The keyboard
![A computer keyboard]({{res:l1-computer-keyboard-teach-computing.png}})

- the buttons on a keyboard are called KEYS
- the letter keys are not in alphabetical order

> 🧑‍🏫 The letters are not in ABC order, and they show as capitals on most keyboards. This matches the "find the keys" label task on the activity worksheet. Point at Space, Backspace and Enter.

## Your turn — type your name
- open the word processor
- press the letter keys to type your name
- then type a friend's name

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: suggest the part of the keyboard a key is in, don't point to the exact key. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Likely error: holding a key so it repeats — fix-words "one short press for one letter."

## I can…
- tick your three "I can…"
- show me your name on the screen

> 🧑‍🏫 Note who can find a letter key on their own. We will not save work this lesson.
