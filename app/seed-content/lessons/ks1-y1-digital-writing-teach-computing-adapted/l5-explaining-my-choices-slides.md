# Explaining my choices

## Today we are learning
- say which tool I used
- use Undo to remove a change
- decide if a change made my writing better

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: undo · redo · font · format. Same routine. Open last lesson's saved poster.

## Which tool am I?
- I make letters dark and thick → Bold
- I make letters lean over → Italic
- I put a line under letters → Underline

> 🧑‍🏫 Read each hint, pupils choose the tool. This matches the starter worksheet riddles.

## The Undo button
![A word processor toolbar showing Undo and Redo]({{res:l5-word-processor-toolbar-undo-and-redo-teach-computing.png}})

- Undo removes your last change
- the arrow points back, like Backspace

> 🧑‍🏫 Paper-fold demo: fold the paper, then "undo" by unfolding. Undo removes the LAST change first. Likely error: expecting Undo to remove an older change directly — fix-words "undo works backwards, one step at a time."

## The Redo button
- Redo puts a change back
- the arrow points the other way

> 🧑‍🏫 Show Redo does the opposite of Undo. Keep it concrete with the paper fold.
