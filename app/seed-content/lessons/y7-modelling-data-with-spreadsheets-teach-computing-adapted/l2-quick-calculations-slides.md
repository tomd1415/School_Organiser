# Quick calculations

## Today we are learning
- write a formula that starts with =
- use cell references in a formula
- use + - * /
- use the autofill tool

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary: drag handle · autofill · formula · cell reference.

## Starter — times tables
![Times-table spreadsheet]({{res:l2-times-table-spreadsheet-teach-computing.png}})

Fill in the 2 times table.

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils answer on the starter worksheet. Draw out: every formula starts with =.

## A formula starts with =
= C20 * D20 means "multiply cell C20 by cell D20".

> 🧑‍🏫 Model typing the formula and pressing Enter. Likely error: forgets the =. Fix-words: "start with equals."

## The four operators
+ add · − subtract · * multiply · / divide

> 🧑‍🏫 Stress * for multiply (not x) and / for divide (not ÷).

## Cell references follow a pattern
= C20*D20, then = C21*D21, then = C22*D22 — only the numbers change.

> 🧑‍🏫 We do: predict the next formula together. The change is always the next-door cell.

## Autofill — the drag handle
Select 2–3 cells, then drag the little handle in the corner down to fill the rest.

> 🧑‍🏫 Model autofill. Support: a printed "drag from here" strip. Challenge: autofill the formula column too.
