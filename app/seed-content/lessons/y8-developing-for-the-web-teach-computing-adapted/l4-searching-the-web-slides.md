## Crawlers and indexing  (I do)
![A crawler following links from page to page]({{res:l4-web-crawler-following-links-teach-computing.png}})

A crawler visits a page, records the keywords and links, stores it in an index, then follows a link to the next page.

> 🧑‍🏫 Steps: read metatags → record keywords + frequency → record hyperlinks → store → follow links. Small words like "the" and "and" are ignored.

## Reading a page like a crawler  (we do)
![An example web page]({{res:l4-example-web-page-teach-computing.png}})

Which words best describe this page? Those are the keywords.

> 🧑‍🏫 Keywords near the top and in the title matter more. This is not an exact science — different engines crawl differently.

## What makes a page rank higher?  (you do)
Clear headings · important keywords near the top · metatags · good images · updated recently.

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: card-sort recorded vs ignored. Core: order the crawler steps + list keywords. Challenge: explain ranking. Make a quality page and screenshot it.
