# What is video?

## Today we are learning
- say what a video is
- spot a talking head and a close up
- compare two videos
- know what to do if something online upsets me

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: video · audio · camera · talking head · close up. Same calm routine as every lesson. This unit builds to each pupil making their own short video.

## Video is moving pictures
![A film clapperboard and a reel of film]({{res:l1-film-clapper.jpg}})

- a **video** is moving pictures
- it usually has **sound** (audio) too
- a photo does NOT move — a video does

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. Ask: "What is a video?" Take a few ideas, do not over-prompt — we come back to it at the end. Likely error: "a video is a photo". Fix-words: "a photo is still, a video MOVES."

## Talking head and close up
![A wide talking-head shot]({{res:l1-talking-head.png}})

- a **talking head** is a wide shot of a person talking
- a **close up** is a very close shot — mostly a face
- we will use these shots in our own videos

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do → we-do. Play ONE short, calm clip (teacher-played, low volume) — pause after about 2 minutes. Point out the talking head and the close up. Pupils match the shots on the activity worksheet.

## Not all videos are the same
- some videos are one person talking to the camera
- some are music videos with lots of scenes and people
- the sound is often added AFTER filming

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 3. Show a second, different clip (teacher-played). Think–pair–share: how is it different from the first one? Pupils write one difference on the activity worksheet.

## Staying safe online
![An age rating badge — 12A]({{res:l1-age-rating.png}})

- videos have **age ratings** — some are not for children
- if something online **upsets you**, tell a **trusted adult**
- a teacher, a parent or carer, or a TA are trusted adults

> 🧑‍🏫 Online safety. Keep it calm and matter-of-fact. Link to the school's online safety rules (e.g. SMART). The main message: tell a trusted adult. Pupils answer the safety questions on the activity worksheet.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Say what a video is — has your answer changed since the start?

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Revisit "What is a video?" from the starter — celebrate improved answers. Check each pupil can point to a talking head and a close up.
