# Using a storyboard

## Today we are learning
- say what a storyboard is
- learn three new techniques
- film a short video from a storyboard
- review how good my video is

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: storyboard · static camera · zoom · pan · tilt. Recap last lesson's shots first (close up, long shot, angles).

## What is a storyboard?
![A camera operator filming]({{res:l3-camera-operator.png}})

- a **storyboard** is a plan for a video
- it shows each shot BEFORE you film
- each box has a picture, the shot type, and the words

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. Many pupils have met storyboards in story planning. Show the example storyboard handout. Explain: today they film a ready-made storyboard so they can focus on the camera, not the plan.

## Three new techniques
- **static camera** — stays still (rest it on books or a stand)
- **zoom** — makes the subject bigger or smaller, camera stays put
- **pan and tilt** — the camera turns to follow

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. Demonstrate each one. Static = newsreader. Zoom = wildlife from far away. Pan/tilt = a tall building or a wide view. Pupils match them on the activity worksheet. Likely error: walking while filming a "static" shot. Fix-words: "static means the camera does not move."

## Film your video
- read the storyboard
- set up → record → act → stop → watch back
- work calmly in your small group

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do (20 min). Groups of 2–3 film the given storyboard. Everyone gets a turn to film and to be filmed (unless agreed otherwise). Pupils order the filming steps on the activity worksheet. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Review your video
- how helpful was the storyboard?
- how good was each scene?
- what would you change next time?

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Pupils rate the storyboard and write one improvement on the activity worksheet. Keep it kind and short. Next lesson: plan your OWN video.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show one scene you filmed.

> 🧑‍🏫 Check each group saved/kept their practice video. Reassure: the real project starts next lesson.
