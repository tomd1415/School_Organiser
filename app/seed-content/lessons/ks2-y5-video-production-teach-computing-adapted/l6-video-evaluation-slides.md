# Video evaluation

## Today we are learning
- trim and reorder my clips
- export my video as a file
- evaluate my video kindly
- think about how to share it safely

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: delete · trim · reorder · export · evaluate · share. Last lesson of the unit — celebrate the whole journey.

## Editing tools
![A film strip being cut with scissors]({{res:l6-trim-scissors.png}})

- **delete** — removes a whole clip
- **trim** — cuts off part of a clip
- **reorder** — moves clips into a new order

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1. Pupils match the tools on the starter worksheet. Demonstrate each one in your editor (or play the example clips, low volume).

## Finish your video
![A video editor screen]({{res:l6-video-editor.png}})

- delete the takes you do not want
- trim off wobbly or boring bits
- put the clips in the order of your storyboard

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1 + 2 (you-do). Pupils finish editing. Likely error: clips out of order. Fix-words: "match the storyboard order." TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Export your video
- **save** keeps your editable project
- **export** makes one finished **mp4** file
- the mp4 plays on any device

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 3. Model exporting to mp4. Pupils order the final steps on the activity worksheet, then export. Watch their finished video back.

## Evaluate and share
- say ONE good thing and ONE kind idea to improve
- to share a video with other people in it: ASK them first
- ask a teacher; keep it private if unsure

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Keep feedback kind and short. Discuss sharing options (a sharing site, email, Bluetooth, USB) and rank them most-private to most-public. Main message: ask before you share other people. Celebrate every finished video.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show your finished, exported video.

> 🧑‍🏫 Celebrate the whole journey: what is video → shots → storyboard → plan → film → edit → export. Well done.
