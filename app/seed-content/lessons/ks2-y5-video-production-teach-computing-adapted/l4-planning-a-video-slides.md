# Planning a video

## Today we are learning
- choose a theme for my video
- plan my scenes on a storyboard
- film and save my first scene

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: storyboard · scene · script · save. This is the start of the real project — same calm routine, more time over the next lessons.

## Choose your theme
- a short video of someone talking to the camera
- with a **prop** (a real object to show)
- like the example video we watched in Lesson 1

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1. Keep themes realistic and props easy to get. Work in pairs or threes; everyone gets to film and be filmed (unless agreed). Pupils write their theme on the activity worksheet.

## Plan your storyboard
![A film clapperboard and a reel of film]({{res:l4-film-clapper.jpg}})

- each box = a **picture**, the **shot type**, and the **words**
- plan ALL your scenes before you film
- the first three boxes are started for you

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 2 (20 min). Hand out the storyboard. Pupils complete the remaining scenes. Likely error: planning too many scenes. Fix-words: "keep it short — a few good scenes." TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Film and save your first scene
- use the first box of your storyboard
- film → watch it back → film again if needed
- **save** your video to the device

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 3. Model where files save on your device. Everyone saves before the end. Pupils order the plan → film → save steps on the activity worksheet.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Show your first scene or your storyboard.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Quick review: is the picture clear? is the sound clear? does it match the storyboard? Reassure: more time to film and improve next lesson. If pupils may bring props, remind them.
