# Filming techniques

## Today we are learning
- find the parts of my filming device
- film using different shots
- match a shot to what it is good for
- spot shots used in a video

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: video camera · lens · record · close up · long shot · high angle · low angle. Same routine as every lesson.

## Get to know your device
- the **lens** is the eye the camera looks through
- the **record light** shows it is filming
- the **screen** shows what the camera sees
- always film in **landscape** (turned sideways)

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. Hand out devices. Let pupils turn on/off, record a few seconds, and play it back so they feel comfortable. Pupils label the camera on the starter worksheet. Likely error: filming in portrait. Fix-words: "turn it sideways — landscape."

## Different shots
![A camera operator filming]({{res:l2-camera-operator.png}})

- **close up** — a face up close
- **long shot** — a whole body and where they are
- **side by side** — two people at the same time

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do → you-do. Demonstrate each shot, then pupils try them in pairs (these are just for practice — not for the project). Keep groups small and calm. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Camera angles
- **high angle** — makes a person look smaller
- **low angle** — makes a person look bigger
- **normal angle** — makes a person look their real size

> 🧑‍🏫 Show each angle. Pupils sort the angles on the activity worksheet. Keep it concrete: stand on a step (high) vs crouch down (low).

## Which technique?
- match the shot to what it shows
- think about what each shot is good for

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 2 + 3. Pupils match shots on the activity worksheet, then watch a short clip (teacher-played) and spot which shots are used. Review answers together.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show one shot you filmed.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Each pupil names one shot and says what it is good for. Save practice clips — next lesson we plan and film from a storyboard.
