# Making groups — building a tree

## Today we are learning
- choose an attribute to split objects into two groups
- split a group again to make smaller groups
- arrange objects and questions into a tree

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: branching database · attribute · question · group · tree · even.

## Split, then split again
- "Can it fly?" splits the vehicles into two groups
- now take the "cannot fly" group: "Does it have an engine?" splits it again

> 🧑‍🏫 Model on the board. A group can be split more than once. Likely error: a second question that leaves an empty group — a good question makes two groups, not an empty one.

## Why is it called a tree?
![A tree with branches]({{res:l2-branching-tree-shape.png}})

- "branching" means it splits like a tree
- a branching database is a tree of yes/no questions

> 🧑‍🏫 Turn the tree upside down: the first question is the trunk, the objects are the leaves.

## A finished branching database
![A minibeast branching database]({{res:l2-minibeast-branching-database.png}})

- pink boxes are QUESTIONS
- blue boxes are OBJECTS (the answers)

> 🧑‍🏫 This matches the label task on the activity worksheet: a first question (top), a question, an object (a leaf).

## Your turn — make a tree
- cut the dinosaur cards
- choose yes/no questions to split them
- keep splitting until each dinosaur is on its own

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: arrange three cards with one given question. Core: choose the questions for all six. Challenge: keep the groups even so the tree is short. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.
