# Planning a branching database

## Today we are learning
- write my own yes/no questions to tell dinosaurs apart
- choose questions that give every dinosaur its own ending
- lay out a branching database plan on paper

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the three "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: attribute · value · question · plan · identify · unique.

## Dinosaur attributes
![Dinosaur cards]({{res:l5-dinosaur-cards-teach-computing.png}})

- main colour
- diet (what it eats)
- where it lives
- length (how long it is)

> 🧑‍🏫 Ask how two dinosaurs are different. The starter sorts attributes from things that are not about the dinosaur.

## Write yes/no questions
- "Does it have wings?"
- "Is it taller than a person?"
- "Does it have spots?"

> 🧑‍🏫 Model writing one question. Likely error: a question every dinosaur answers the same way — fix-words: a useful question gets a yes from some and a no from others.

## Lay out your plan
- arrange the dinosaur cards and questions on paper
- split until each dinosaur is on its own
- test the plan with a partner

> 🧑‍🏫 Support: finish a sentence-started question. Core: write three different questions. Challenge: make sure every dinosaur ends up on its own. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## I can…
Tick your three "I can…". Show me your first question and your paper plan.

> 🧑‍🏫 Keep the plans safe — pupils build them online next lesson.
