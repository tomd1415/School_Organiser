# Planning a podcast

## Today we are learning
- add a sound effect on its own track (a layer)
- move and set the volume of a track
- save my project so I can keep editing it
- plan the parts of my podcast

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: audio · layer · import · save · host · engineer. Same routine as every lesson.

## Layers of sound
![Three tracks layered: music, voice and a sound effect]({{res:l3-layered-tracks-music-voice-sound-effect-teach-computing.png}})

- each sound goes on its own **track**
- stacking them is called **layering**
- layers make a podcast more interesting to listen to

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. Show the three named tracks. Play them together, then mute one (teacher-played). Pupils label the tracks on the starter worksheet.

## Add a sound effect
- record a short voice track
- **import** a sound effect
- **move** it to the right place
- listen, then change the volume if needed

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do → we-do. Model import → move → listen. Likely error: forgetting to listen after each change. Fix-words: "change one thing, then listen." Set volume so the voice is clear.

## Save your project
- **save** keeps all your tracks separate
- so you can open it and edit it again next time
- save often

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Everyone saves their project before moving on. TA: check each pupil's project is saved with their name.

## Plan your podcast
- what is it about?
- who talks in each part?
- where will music or sound effects go?
- how does it start and end?

> 🧑‍🏫 Pupils fill the plan on the activity worksheet. "School news" is the example, but any topic is fine. Agree who is host and who is engineer; everyone gets a turn.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show your plan and your saved project.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Check each pupil has a saved project and a plan for next lesson.
