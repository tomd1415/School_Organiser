# Creating a podcast

## Today we are learning
- record my voice tracks from my plan
- review my recordings and spot what to fix
- trim and line up my voice tracks
- save my project to finish later

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: record · playback · edit · selection · waveform · audio engineer. Same routine as every lesson.

## The Audacity editor
![The Audacity editor with the record button, play button, timeline and tracks]({{res:l4-the-audacity-editor-teach-computing.png}})

- **record** button — the red circle
- **play** button — the green triangle
- a **track** holds one sound's waveform
- the **timeline** shows the time across the top

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. Point to each part. Pupils label the editor on the starter worksheet. Likely error: pressing record over an old track. Fix-words: "mute the old track, then record a new one."

## Record from your plan
- open your saved project and your plan
- record each part on its own track
- mute a track before recording the next one
- it is fine to take a few tries

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do → you-do (20 min). Model record → play → delete. Computers let us try again easily — that is a strength. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Record in a calm spot.

## Review your recordings
- listen to each track
- is it clear? any long pauses? any mistakes?
- note what you want to change

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do. Use mute/solo to hear one track at a time. An audio engineer uses the waveform to spot coughs and bangs (big sudden spikes).

## Trim, line up and save
- trim mistakes and long pauses
- line up the tracks so they play in order
- **save** your project to finish next lesson

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Everyone saves before the end. Reassure: more time next lesson to add music and sound effects.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show your voice tracks.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Check each pupil has saved their project with their voice tracks recorded.
