# Evaluating podcasts

## Today we are learning
- listen carefully and say what is good about a podcast
- suggest one way to improve a podcast
- make a change to improve my own podcast
- export my finished podcast as an MP3

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: evaluate · feedback · export · MP3 · improve. Same routine as every lesson.

## Listen carefully
![Listening back to a podcast]({{res:l6-listening-back-to-a-podcast-teach-computing.png}})

- listening carefully is a skill
- notice what works well
- notice what could be better

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. Play one short clip (teacher-played, low volume) and ask pupils to count how many times they hear one word. Builds active listening calmly.

## What makes a podcast good?
- a clear voice
- music and sounds that fit the topic
- no long silent gaps
- one person speaking at a time

> 🧑‍🏫 Agree the class success list. This becomes the checklist pupils use to evaluate.

## Evaluate — be kind and helpful
- listen to your own podcast
- listen to one other group's
- write what is good
- write one kind suggestion

> 🧑‍🏫 Activity 1. Pupils move calmly between computers and fill the evaluation sheet. Model giving feedback that is specific and kind. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## Make one improvement
- read the feedback
- choose one change
- make it, then save and export

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do. Keep it to one clear change so it stays calm and achievable. Remind: save the project, THEN export the MP3.

## You made a podcast!
- you recorded, edited, layered and exported
- well done

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Celebrate the whole journey: record → edit → layer → evaluate → export. Tick the four "I can…".
