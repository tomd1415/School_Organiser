# Editing audio

## Today we are learning
- record my voice again to make it better
- read the waveform to find loud and quiet parts
- trim and delete parts of a recording
- name some features of a podcast

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: audio · podcast · edit · trim · waveform · track. Same routine as every lesson.

## The waveform
![A waveform: tall parts are loud, flat parts are quiet]({{res:l2-waveform-loud-and-quiet-teach-computing.png}})

- Audacity draws a picture of your sound
- tall, fat parts = **loud**
- thin, flat line = **quiet** or silence

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. Play a short recording and watch the waveform move (teacher-played). Pupils label the waveform on the starter worksheet. Aim for the wave to reach about +0.5 / −0.5 for a clear voice.

## Editing means changing
- on a computer you can change a recording after you make it
- one way is to **trim** — take a part off
- you can delete mistakes or long quiet bits

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do. Model: select → listen → delete → listen again. Likely error: deleting without listening first. Fix-words: "select, listen, THEN delete."

## Trim a part
- look at the waveform to find the part
- **select** it with the mouse
- **listen** to check it is the right bit
- press **delete**, then listen again

> 🧑‍🏫 We-do → you-do. Pupils order these steps on the activity worksheet, then trim their own recording. TA: prompt, do not do it for them.

## What is a podcast?
- a recording people listen to, like a radio show you can play any time
- it can have voices, music and sound effects
- a person who edits sound for a job is an **audio engineer**

> 🧑‍🏫 Play one short, calm podcast clip (teacher-played — keep the volume low). Ask: what did you hear? Build a class list of podcast features.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show one part you trimmed.

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Check each pupil can point to a loud and a quiet part on their own waveform.
