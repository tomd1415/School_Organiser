# Recording sound

## Today we are learning
- name the device that records sound
- name the devices that play sound
- record my own voice on a computer
- say who is allowed to use a recording

> 🧑‍🏫 Read the four "I can…" aloud. Vocabulary on the board: audio · microphone · speaker · headphones · input device · output device. Same routine as every lesson.

## Sound goes IN and OUT
![A microphone records sound]({{res:l1-microphone.png}})

- a **microphone** records sound IN — it is an **input** device
- a **speaker** or **headphones** play sound OUT — they are **output** devices

> 🧑‍🏫 Starter. Hold up the real devices if you have them. Ask: which one do you TALK into? Which one do you LISTEN with? Likely error: mixing up input and output. Fix-words: "IN to record, OUT to hear."

## Some devices do both
![Headphones play sound out]({{res:l1-headphones.png}})

- a **headset** has a microphone AND ear cups
- so it is BOTH input and output
- a laptop has a built-in microphone and speaker too

> 🧑‍🏫 I-do → we-do. Point out the pinhole microphone and the speaker grille on a laptop. Pupils sort the devices on the activity worksheet (input vs output).

## Record your voice
- open Audacity
- press the red **record** button and say one sentence
- press **stop**, then **play** to hear it
- do not like it? **delete** and try again

> 🧑‍🏫 You-do (15 min). Model record → play → delete first. Keep recordings to one sentence so everyone gets a turn. TA: prompt, do not do it for them. Record in a calm spot with little background noise.

## Who owns a recording?
- the person who records the sound made it
- they choose who is allowed to use it
- ask before you use someone else's sound

> 🧑‍🏫 Plenary. Quick discussion: if you recorded a friend, you should ask them before sharing it. Link to copyright: people own what they make.

## I can…
Tick your four "I can…". Show one recording you are proud of.

> 🧑‍🏫 Check each pupil can point to the microphone and a speaker, and has one recording saved.
